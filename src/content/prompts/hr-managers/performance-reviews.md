## Annual Performance Reviews

> You are an HR manager writing a year-end performance review for a high-performing employee being considered for promotion.
> 
> Employee: {employee_name}
> Role: {job_title}, {years_at_company} years at the company
> Rating: {exceeds_expectations / meets_expectations / below_expectations}
> Three major wins this year: {specific_win_1}, {specific_win_2}, {specific_win_3}
> Two growth areas: {growth_area_1}, {growth_area_2}
> Promotion consideration: {yes / no / not_yet}
> Company tone: {formal / conversational / direct}
> 
> Write a 400-450 word performance review using the SBI model (Situation, Behaviour, Impact). Open with recognition that names their biggest win. Address growth areas in the middle third, framed as development opportunities. Close with promotion recommendation and two specific goals for next year.

When to use it: When you're 48 hours from the promotion committee meeting and need a compelling case for advancement.

Pro tip: If promotion consideration is "not_yet", replace the closing with a timeline like "ready for promotion consideration in 12-18 months with development in X and Y areas."

## Performance Improvement Plans

> You are creating a 90-day performance improvement plan for an employee with persistent quality issues.
> 
> Employee: {employee_name}
> Position: {job_title}
> Tenure: {years_months}
> Specific quality issues: {error_types_or_examples}
> Impact on team/customers: {specific_consequences}
> Previous feedback given: {verbal_warnings / written_warning / coaching_attempts}
> Root cause assessment: {skill_gap / attention_to_detail / workload / training_needed}
> Manager support available: {daily_check_ins / weekly_reviews / training_resources}
> Success measurement: {error_rate / quality_score / customer_feedback}
> 
> Write a detailed 90-day improvement plan with weekly milestones, specific behavioral expectations, support resources, and clear consequences. Include exact metrics for success. Use firm but supportive language.

When to use it: When informal coaching isn't working and you need formal documentation before considering termination.

Pro tip: Set weekly check-ins for the first 30 days, then bi-weekly—early intensive support shows good faith effort and catches problems quickly.

## Mid-Year Check-ins

> You are conducting a mid-year review conversation with a high performer who's showing signs of burnout.
> 
> Employee: {employee_name}
> Performance level: {consistently_exceeds / recently_declining}
> Workload indicators: {overtime_hours / project_count / stress_signs}
> Personal situation: {family_changes / health_issues / life_events / unknown}
> Team dependencies: {how_critical_they_are}
> Burnout signals: {fatigue / irritability / quality_decline / disengagement}
> Support options: {workload_redistribution / time_off / resource_addition}
> Employee openness: {shares_concerns / minimizes_issues / defensive}
> 
> Write talking points for a 30-minute mid-year conversation that acknowledges their contributions, directly addresses burnout concerns, and collaboratively develops a sustainability plan. Include specific questions to ask and support options to offer.

When to use it: When your star performer is working unsustainable hours and you need to address it before they burn out or quit.

Pro tip: Lead with appreciation for their work, then express concern for their wellbeing—it frames the conversation as care, not performance criticism.

## Exit and Transition Reviews

> You are conducting an exit interview with a high-performing employee who's leaving for a better opportunity.
> 
> Departing employee: {employee_name}
> Tenure: {years_months}
> Performance level: {high / exceptional}
> Reason for leaving: {career_advancement / compensation / company_culture / management / location}
> New role details: {promotion / lateral_move / industry_change / startup / competitor}
> Relationship with manager: {positive / neutral / strained}
> Team impact: {significant_loss / manageable / succession_planned}
> Feedback willingness: {very_open / somewhat_guarded / professional_only}
> 
> Write an exit interview conversation guide that gathers actionable feedback about retention factors, management effectiveness, and process improvements. Include questions that encourage honest feedback while maintaining positive relationships. Focus on organizational learning rather than changing their mind.

When to use it: When valuable employees leave and you need to understand systemic issues that might cause others to follow.

Pro tip: Ask "What would have needed to change for you to stay?" rather than "Why are you leaving?"—it gives more actionable information for preventing future departures.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the difference between using ChatGPT vs dedicated HR software for performance reviews?
ChatGPT helps you write the actual review content quickly, while HR software manages the process, storage, and workflow. Use ChatGPT to draft the narrative sections, then paste them into your HR system. It's faster than starting with a blank text box.

### How do I customize these prompts if my company uses a different performance framework than SBI or STAR?
Replace the framework reference with your company's method. If you use a 9-box grid, add "categorize as {high_potential / core_contributor / inconsistent_performer}" as a variable. The prompt structure works with any framework.

### Can I use these prompts for reviews in different countries with different employment laws?
These prompts focus on performance documentation, which is generally similar across regions. However, always have your legal team review final documents for local compliance, especially improvement plans and termination documentation.
