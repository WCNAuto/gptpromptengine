## Template structure

### Campaign Master Database
- **Campaign Name (Title)**: "Q3 Product Launch - Enterprise CRM" or "Holiday 2026 - Email + Social". The name that appears in all status reports and client communications.
- **Launch Date (Date)**: Final go-live date for the entire campaign. Everything else calculates backward from this date—get it wrong and all task deadlines shift.
- **Campaign Stage (Select)**: Planning / Pre-production / Production / Launch / Post-campaign. Drives which tasks show up in your weekly focus view.
- **Campaign Manager (Person)**: Single point of contact who owns the launch timeline. Gets tagged in all overdue task notifications.
- **Budget (Number)**: Total approved spend across all channels. Empty budget blocks the finance approval workflow view.
- **Channel Mix (Multi-select)**: Paid Social / Email / Content / PR / Events / Paid Search. Determines which deliverable templates auto-populate when you create the campaign.

### Campaign Deliverables Database
- **Deliverable Name (Title)**: "Landing page wireframes" or "Instagram creative batch 1 (5 assets)". Specific enough that the assignee knows exactly what to deliver.
- **Assignee (Person)**: Single owner responsible for completion. Multiple people create accountability gaps—assign one person who can delegate internally.
- **Due Date (Date)**: Hard deadline for completion, not "nice to have" dates. Set based on dependencies and launch date—most deliverables need 3-5 days buffer before campaign launch.
- **Status (Select)**: Not started / In progress / Review / Approved / Blocked. Change to Blocked the moment a dependency isn't cleared—drives the weekly escalation report.
- **Campaign (Relation)**: Links to Campaign Master Database. Empty relation means the task won't appear in any campaign-specific views.
- **Deliverable Type (Select)**: Creative / Copy / Technical / Legal / Strategy. Determines which review template and approval workflow applies.
- **Priority (Select)**: Critical path / Important / Nice to have. Critical path items get flagged in red when they hit 48 hours before due date.

## How to use it week by week

1. **Monday morning campaign setup**: Paste the new campaign brief into Campaign Name, set Launch Date, and select Channel Mix. The template auto-creates deliverable templates based on your channel selection.

2. **Tuesday task assignment**: Open the Unassigned Tasks view, assign each deliverable to an Assignee, and set Due Date working backward from Launch Date. Set anything blocking launch day as Critical path Priority.

3. **Wednesday dependency mapping**: For any task marked Blocked, add the blocking reason in the notes and tag the person who needs to unblock it. These surface in Thursday's escalation review.

4. **Friday status reporting**: Open the Weekly Status Report view, which auto-groups by Campaign and Status. Copy this view and paste into client emails—shows exactly what's on track, what's overdue, and what's blocking launch.

5. **When deliverables come in**: Change Status to Review the moment someone submits work. Change to Approved only when you have written sign-off in the notes field—verbal approval doesn't count and leads to scope creep later.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why does the Priority field only show three options instead of numbered priorities?
More than three priority levels create decision paralysis when assigning tasks. Critical path covers anything that blocks launch if delayed. Important covers deliverables that impact campaign success but don't stop launch. Nice to have covers optimization tasks that can be pushed to the next campaign if needed.

### How do I handle campaigns with different approval workflows for creative vs copy?
The Deliverable Type field drives different filtered views for each approval track. Creative deliverables get routed to the Visual Review view which includes brand guidelines checklist. Copy deliverables use the Copy Review view with legal and compliance checks. Both link back to the main Campaign view once approved.

### Can I track campaign performance after launch or just the planning phase?
This template stops at launch day—it's built for delivery tracking, not performance analysis. Once Status changes to Approved for all deliverables, archive the campaign and move performance tracking to your analytics dashboard. Mixing planning and performance data in one template creates confusion about what needs action.
