## Template structure

### Content Pipeline Database
- **Video Title** (Title): "How to Fix Audio Sync Issues in DaVinci Resolve" — the exact title that appears on YouTube. This field drives the URL slug formula and the calendar view sorting.
- **Status** (Select): Idea / Script Draft / Script Final / Filmed / Edited / Thumbnail Done / Scheduled / Published. Move to Script Final only when you can film without referring to notes. The "Ready to Film" view filters on Script Final status.
- **Upload Date** (Date): 2026-06-15 — the exact date this video goes live. Leave empty for idea-stage videos. This field drives the calendar view and gap detection.
- **Video Type** (Select): Tutorial / Review / Vlog / List / Interview / Behind-scenes. Determines which template you pull for scripting and affects the time estimates in Duration formula.
- **Priority** (Select): Must film / Should film / Nice to have / Archive. Set to Must film for videos tied to trends, product launches, or seasonal content with hard deadlines.
- **Script Status** (Text): "Intro done, need 3 more examples for section 2" — specific blockers or next actions. Critical for the weekly planning view.
- **Thumbnail Concept** (Text): "Split screen: broken audio waveform vs clean waveform, red arrow pointing to sync button" — enough detail that your designer can execute without a call.
- **Keywords Target** (Text): "davinci resolve audio, fix audio sync, video editing tutorial" — the 3-5 keywords this video targets for search.

### Content Calendar View
Groups videos by Upload Date in timeline format. Shows only videos with Status of Filmed or later. This view goes to your editor every Monday with the week's deliverables.

### Ready to Film View
Filters on Script Final status and Must film priority. This view determines your filming schedule and prevents bottlenecks.

## How to use it week by week

1. **Sunday evening**: Open Ready to Film view. Block filming time for every video showing Must film priority. If fewer than 3 videos are ready, bump 2 videos from Should film to Must film and finish their scripts.

2. **Monday morning**: Check Content Calendar view for upload gaps in the next 21 days. Any week with fewer than your target uploads gets flagged. Move idea-stage videos to Script Draft status to fill gaps.

3. **Wednesday after filming**: Update Status to Filmed for completed videos. Add any script changes or filming notes to Script Status field. Set Upload Date based on your editing queue.

4. **Friday afternoon**: Review Thumbnail Concept field for next week's videos. Send concepts to designer or add to your creation queue. Update Status to Scheduled once videos are uploaded with publish dates set.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I handle series or multi-part videos in the Video Type field?
Add custom options like "Series Part 1", "Series Part 2" to the Video Type select. Each video gets its own database entry with sequential Upload Dates. Use the Keywords Target field to link them: "productivity series part 1, morning routine, productivity tips".

### What if my upload schedule changes and I need to shift multiple videos?
Select all affected videos in the Content Calendar view, then bulk edit the Upload Date property. The calendar automatically reflows and the gap detection updates. Always leave 2-3 buffer days between videos to absorb delays.

### Should I track analytics or performance data in this template?
No. This template handles pre-publication workflow only. Once Status hits Published, your analytics platform takes over. Adding performance tracking here clutters the planning views and slows down your weekly workflow.
