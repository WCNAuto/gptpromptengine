## Template structure

### Launch Tasks Database
- **Task Name (Title)**: "Influencer partnership contracts signed" or "Amazon listing live with A+ content" — the specific deliverable, not the work stream
- **Owner (Person)**: Assigned team member who reports status and unblocks dependencies. Every task needs exactly one owner or it falls through cracks during crunch time
- **Function (Select)**: Marketing / Operations / Product / Partnerships / Legal — determines which filtered view this task appears in for daily standups
- **Status (Select)**: Not started / In progress / Blocked / Review needed / Complete — change to Blocked the moment you hit a dependency, triggers automatic escalation in weekly reports
- **Due Date (Date)**: Hard deadline for this deliverable. Drives the Timeline view and auto-calculates days overdue for executive dashboard
- **Launch Week (Select)**: Week -8 to Week +2 — when this deliverable must be complete relative to launch day, keeps timeline realistic during scope creep
- **Dependencies (Relation)**: Links to other tasks that must finish first. Essential for identifying critical path when launch date moves
- **Notes (Text)**: Current blockers, vendor contacts, or approval status. Updated during weekly reviews, feeds into stakeholder reports

### Weekly Status Reports Page
Pre-formatted template for executive updates showing completed tasks, current blockers, and revised timeline. Copy-paste ready for investor updates or board decks.

## How to use it week by week

1. **Monday of launch planning**: Import your existing task list into Task Name field, assign every Owner from your team dropdown, set all Due Dates based on your launch target date
2. **Every Tuesday during launch cycle**: Each Owner updates Status for their tasks during morning standup, adds any new Dependencies discovered, documents current blockers in Notes field
3. **Every Friday afternoon**: Review Overdue Tasks view, escalate any Blocked items to executive team, copy Weekly Status Report page and update with this week's completed/delayed items
4. **Two weeks before launch**: Switch to Timeline view sorted by Due Date, identify critical path through Dependencies, move any non-essential tasks to post-launch weeks
5. **Launch week**: Use Today filter to see only tasks due this week, check Dependencies view to ensure no surprise blockers, update stakeholder report with go/no-go decision items

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I handle dependencies between tasks when launch dates shift?
The Dependencies relation field automatically updates all linked tasks when you change a due date. If "Influencer contracts signed" moves from Week -4 to Week -3, all dependent tasks like "Influencer content review" get flagged in the Overdue Tasks view. Update dependent due dates manually — the template shows the cascade but doesn't auto-reschedule to avoid breaking other launch deadlines.

### What's the difference between Blocked and Review needed status?
Set to Blocked when you're waiting on an external dependency like vendor approval, legal review, or another team's deliverable. Set to Review needed when your work is complete but needs internal sign-off. Blocked items appear in executive escalation reports; Review needed items appear in your manager's weekly approval queue.

### Can I track multiple product launches simultaneously?
Duplicate the entire template for each launch and rename the database. Don't use one template for multiple launches — the Timeline view becomes unusable and Dependencies between different product launches create false critical paths. Each launch gets its own workspace section with this template structure.
