## What this template does

Replaces the manual process of tracking customer health across spreadsheets, CRM notes, and email threads. When you open it day one, you see a master account database with health scores, renewal dates, and risk flags for every customer. After one week of logging interactions, the automated views surface your 5 highest-risk accounts and generate a weekly executive summary you send directly to leadership. Tracks up to 50 active accounts with 12 predefined success milestones per customer.

## Template structure

### Account Health Dashboard
- **Account Name** (Title): Full customer company name exactly as it appears in your CRM
- **Health Score** (Select): Green / Yellow / Red. Update every Monday based on engagement metrics and milestone progress
- **Renewal Date** (Date): Exact contract renewal date. Drives the 30/60/90 day warning views that prioritise your outreach
- **MRR** (Number): Monthly recurring revenue. Leave empty and the Risk Assessment view won't calculate churn impact correctly
- **Primary Contact** (Text): Full name and email of your main stakeholder. Update immediately when contacts change or you lose access
- **Stage** (Select): Onboarding / Active / Renewal / Expansion / Churn Risk. Change to Churn Risk the moment they miss two consecutive check-ins

### Success Milestones
- **Milestone** (Title): First Login / Implementation Complete / First Value Achieved / Team Trained / Advanced Features Adopted
- **Due Date** (Date): Target completion date set during kickoff. Overdue milestones automatically appear in your weekly risk report
- **Status** (Select): Not Started / In Progress / Blocked / Complete. Set to Blocked when waiting on customer action for more than 5 business days
- **Account** (Relation): Links to Account Health Dashboard. Required field that enables milestone tracking per customer
- **Owner** (Select): Your name / Customer / Implementation Team. Clarifies who is responsible for moving this milestone forward

### Interaction Log
- **Date** (Date): Exact date of call, email, or meeting. Drives the Last Contact calculation that flags dormant accounts
- **Type** (Select): Check-in Call / Support Issue / QBR / Training / Escalation. QBR entries auto-populate the quarterly health assessment
- **Summary** (Text): 2-3 sentence recap including customer sentiment and next steps. Empty summaries break the executive report generator
- **Account** (Relation): Links to Account Health Dashboard
- **Follow-up Required** (Checkbox): Check when you promised a deliverable or follow-up action. Unchecked items older than 3 days trigger task alerts

## How to use it week by week

1. **Sunday night**: Open the Renewal Pipeline view and review all accounts with renewals in the next 90 days. Update Health Scores based on the week's interactions and milestone progress.

2. **Monday morning**: Log any customer interactions from the previous week in the Interaction Log. Set Follow-up Required checkbox for any promises you made. Check the Overdue Milestones view and send reminder emails.

3. **Wednesday**: Update milestone Status for any customer deliverables completed. Change Account Stage to Churn Risk for any customer that missed scheduled calls or hasn't engaged with the platform in 14+ days.

4. **Friday afternoon**: Export the Executive Summary view and send to your manager. This auto-populates customer health distribution, at-risk MRR total, and overdue milestone count. Use the High Risk Accounts view to plan next week's priority outreach.

5. **Month-end**: Complete QBR entries in the Interaction Log for all scheduled quarterly reviews. Update Renewal Date field for any customers whose contracts were extended or modified during QBR discussions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why does the Health Score view break when I don't fill in MRR values?
The automated Risk Assessment calculation multiplies Health Score by MRR to prioritise which red accounts deserve immediate attention. Without MRR data, a small customer and enterprise client show equal priority. Enter even estimated MRR values to make the prioritisation work.

### How do I track accounts that don't follow the standard 5-milestone framework?
Add custom milestone entries in the Success Milestones database for accounts with unique onboarding needs. The Account relation field links them properly, and they'll appear in the same Overdue Milestones view. Don't modify the milestone Select options — just create additional milestone records.

### Can I use this template for accounts managed by multiple CSMs?
Add a CSM Owner field to the Account Health Dashboard and filter all views by your name. The Interaction Log already supports multiple team members logging touches to the same account. Share the template with your team but create personal filtered views for your account portfolio.
