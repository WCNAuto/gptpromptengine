This template eliminates the chaos of managing an executive's day across multiple apps, email threads, and sticky notes. It produces a single dashboard that shows today's priorities, this week's deliverables, and any items waiting on your executive's input. ## What this template does

It replaces the manual process of checking calendar apps, email, multiple task lists, and project status meetings to figure out what needs your executive's attention right now. When you open it on day one, you see a clean dashboard with sections for today's schedule, priority items, and pending deliverables. After one week of use, it automatically surfaces the 5 most urgent items that need executive action, tracks up to 15 active projects, and generates a weekly briefing you can send every Friday afternoon.

## Template structure

### Daily Dashboard
- **Executive Name** (Text): Full name as it appears in calendar invites and email signatures. Used to auto-populate meeting agendas and status reports.
- **Date** (Date): Today's date. Update every morning when you first open the template to filter all views correctly.
- **Priority Level** (Select): Critical / High / Medium / Low. Set to Critical only for items that must happen today or the executive's schedule breaks tomorrow.
- **Action Required** (Select): Exec decision / Exec approval / Exec attendance / Exec review. Determines which items appear in the "Needs Executive" filtered view.

### Task Management
- **Task** (Text): Specific deliverable or action item. Example: "Review Q3 budget proposal draft" not "budget stuff". Empty tasks break the priority sorting formula.
- **Owner** (Person): Who is responsible for completion. Set to your executive's name only when they must personally do the work, not just approve it.
- **Due Date** (Date): Hard deadline. Leave empty only for ongoing/maintenance tasks. Missing due dates remove items from all time-based views.
- **Status** (Select): Not started / In progress / Waiting on exec / Blocked / Complete. Change to "Waiting on exec" the moment you need their input to proceed.
- **Project** (Relation): Links to Projects database. Required for all tasks except one-off requests that take under 30 minutes.

### Project Tracker
- **Project Name** (Text): Client name + project type. Example: "Johnson Industries office relocation" or "Q4 board deck preparation".
- **Next Deadline** (Date): The next milestone that affects your executive's calendar. Updates whenever a task in this project changes status.
- **Executive Involvement** (Select): Decision maker / Final approver / Informed only / Not involved. Determines which projects appear in executive briefings.
- **Health Status** (Formula): Auto-calculates based on overdue tasks and approaching deadlines. Red = 2+ overdue items, Yellow = deadline within 3 days, Green = on track.

## How to use it week by week

1. **Every Monday at 8 AM**: Open the Daily Dashboard, update the Date field to today, and review the "This Week" filtered view. Add any new projects or tasks from weekend emails before your executive arrives.

2. **Each morning before your executive's first meeting**: Update all "Waiting on exec" tasks based on decisions made yesterday. Set Priority Level to Critical for anything that affects today's meetings or tomorrow's calendar.

3. **After each meeting where action items are assigned**: Create new tasks with specific Due Dates and assign to the correct Project. Set Owner to yourself unless your executive must personally complete the work.

4. **Every Friday at 3 PM**: Export the "Executive Summary" view and send it as next week's priority briefing. Mark completed tasks as "Complete" and update Next Deadline for any projects that hit milestones.

5. **When someone requests executive time**: Check the Health Status in Project Tracker first. Never schedule decision meetings for Red projects without clearing blockers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I handle recurring tasks like weekly reports?
Create one task with the project name "Recurring Operations" and update the Due Date each week instead of creating new tasks. The Status should reset to "Not started" every Monday morning when you update the Date field in the Daily Dashboard.

### What if my executive works on projects I don't have visibility into?
Use Executive Involvement = "Informed only" and leave the Task details minimal. Focus on tracking only the deadlines or meetings that affect their calendar or require your support. The Health Status formula will still flag timing issues.

### Can I track multiple executives with this template?
The database structure supports multiple executives, but the filtered views assume one person. Duplicate the entire template for each executive you support, or add an "Executive" select field and modify the view filters to separate their items.
