## Client Assessment Reports

> You are a business coach writing a comprehensive client assessment report after initial discovery sessions.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Company: {company_name}
> Industry: {industry}
> Current revenue: {annual_revenue}
> Team size: {employee_count}
> Primary challenge: {main_business_problem}
> Secondary challenges: {two_additional_issues}
> Client's stated goals: {three_key_objectives}
> Assessment period: {timeframe_of_discovery}
> 
> Write a 600-800 word assessment report using this structure: Executive Summary (current state in 2-3 sentences), Key Findings (3 main observations with supporting evidence), Opportunity Areas (ranked by impact and feasibility), and Recommended Next Steps (3 specific actions with timelines). Use professional but approachable language. Include one data point or metric in each Key Finding.

**When to use it:** After completing discovery calls with a potential long-term coaching client who needs a formal assessment before committing to an engagement.

**Pro tip:** If your client operates in a highly regulated industry, add "{regulatory_considerations}" as a variable and mention compliance factors in your Key Findings section.

> You are a business coach conducting a leadership readiness assessment for a client considering a promotion.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Current role: {current_position}
> Target role: {desired_position}
> Years in current role: {tenure}
> Team size they'd manage: {future_team_size}
> Key strengths observed: {three_leadership_strengths}
> Development areas: {two_skill_gaps}
> Stakeholder feedback themes: {feedback_summary}
> Assessment tools used: {assessment_methods}
> 
> Write a 400-500 word leadership readiness report. Start with a clear recommendation (ready now / ready with development / not ready). Support with specific examples from your observations. Include a 90-day development plan with 3 concrete actions. End with success metrics for the transition.

**When to use it:** When an existing coaching client is being considered for promotion and their company requires a formal leadership assessment.

**Pro tip:** Frame development areas as "growth accelerators" rather than weaknesses - it positions the promotion as an opportunity to stretch rather than a risk.

> You are a business coach preparing a quarterly progress report for an executive coaching client.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Coaching focus: {primary_development_area}
> Quarter: {q1_q2_q3_q4} {year}
> Sessions completed: {session_count}
> Key breakthrough: {biggest_progress_moment}
> Skill improvement: {specific_skill_developed}
> Behavioral change: {new_behavior_adopted}
> Business impact: {measurable_outcome}
> Remaining challenge: {ongoing_struggle}
> 
> Write a 300-400 word quarterly progress report. Lead with the business impact achieved. Describe the key breakthrough using the client's own words where possible. Address the remaining challenge as "next quarter focus" with one specific action plan. Include a confidence rating (1-10) for the client achieving their annual goal.

**When to use it:** For formal coaching engagements where you need to report progress to HR, the client's manager, or the client themselves.

**Pro tip:** Use the client's language and examples rather than coaching jargon - it makes the progress feel more authentic and personal.

> You are a business coach writing a 360-degree feedback summary report for a senior executive.
> 
> Executive: {executive_name}
> Role: {position}
> Direct reports surveyed: {direct_report_count}
> Peers surveyed: {peer_count}
> Top strength (consistent across groups): {unanimous_strength}
> Leadership blind spot: {development_area}
> Feedback theme from direct reports: {team_feedback}
> Feedback theme from peers: {peer_feedback}
> Self-assessment gap: {where_self_perception_differs}
> 
> Write a 500-600 word 360 feedback report. Start with the consistent strength and why it matters. Present the blind spot as discoverable insight, not criticism. Include one direct quote theme (anonymized). End with two behavioral experiments the executive can try immediately. Use coaching language, not evaluation language.

**When to use it:** When delivering 360 feedback results as part of an executive development program.

**Pro tip:** If there's a significant gap between self-perception and others' feedback, spend extra time on that section - it's usually where the biggest coaching opportunity lies.

## Strategic Planning Reports

> You are a business coach facilitating a strategic planning session and writing the outcomes report.
> 
> Company: {company_name}
> Planning period: {timeframe}
> Current annual revenue: {revenue}
> Revenue target: {growth_target}
> Market position: {competitive_position}
> Core strength to leverage: {key_advantage}
> Primary growth strategy: {chosen_strategy}
> Resource constraint: {biggest_limitation}
> Success metric: {primary_kpi}
> 
> Write a 600-700 word strategic planning summary. Open with the growth target and timeline. Detail the chosen strategy with rationale. Address the resource constraint with a mitigation plan. Include 3 quarterly milestones and who owns each. End with the single most important decision the leadership team must make in the next 30 days.

**When to use it:** After facilitating a strategic planning retreat or multi-session planning process for a coaching client.

**Pro tip:** Always include owner names for milestones - vague accountability kills strategic plans faster than bad strategy.

> You are a business coach helping a client evaluate a significant business decision.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Decision: {specific_decision}
> Option A: {first_choice}
> Option B: {second_choice}
> Financial impact of A: {cost_or_revenue_a}
> Financial impact of B: {cost_or_revenue_b}
> Client's gut instinct: {preferred_option}
> Biggest risk: {primary_concern}
> Success factor: {what_must_go_right}
> 
> Write a 400-500 word decision analysis report. Present both options objectively with pros and cons. Address the biggest risk with a contingency approach. Validate or challenge the client's gut instinct with logical reasoning. End with a clear recommendation and the first step to implement it within 48 hours.

**When to use it:** When a coaching client faces a major business decision and needs structured analysis before committing.

**Pro tip:** If the financial impacts are similar, focus heavily on strategic fit and the client's capacity to execute - most decisions fail on implementation, not concept.

## Performance Improvement Reports

> You are a business coach writing a performance improvement plan after identifying operational inefficiencies.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Department: {affected_department}
> Performance gap: {specific_inefficiency}
> Current metrics: {baseline_performance}
> Target metrics: {desired_performance}
> Root cause: {underlying_issue}
> Quick win opportunity: {immediate_improvement}
> Resource requirement: {needed_investment}
> Timeline: {improvement_period}
> 
> Write a 400-500 word performance improvement report. Open with the performance gap in quantifiable terms. Explain the root cause with supporting evidence. Detail the quick win with implementation steps. Present the full improvement plan in phases with weekly checkpoints. Include success metrics and early warning indicators.

**When to use it:** When coaching clients through operational performance issues that require structured improvement plans.

**Pro tip:** Always start with a quick win that shows improvement within 2 weeks - it builds momentum for the longer-term changes.
