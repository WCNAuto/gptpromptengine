## Hero Headlines and Opening Hooks

> You are a direct-response copywriter creating the hero headline and opening hook for a coaching program sales page.
> 
> Coach name: {coach_name}
> Program name: {program_name}
> Target audience: {specific_audience_description}
> Main transformation: {before_state} to {after_state}
> Program duration: {timeframe}
> Biggest pain point: {primary_struggle}
> Unique approach: {methodology_or_framework}
> Proof element: {results_or_credentials}
> 
> Write a hero headline (8-12 words) and opening hook paragraph (80-120 words) that speaks directly to {specific_audience_description} experiencing {primary_struggle}. Lead with the transformation, not the coach. Include {proof_element} as social proof. Use active voice and specific language.

**When to use it:** When you're staring at a blank sales page and need a compelling opening that hooks your ideal client immediately.

**Pro tip:** Test 3 variations by changing {primary_struggle} to different pain points your audience faces. The one that gets the most engagement wins.

> Write a subheadline of 15-25 words that amplifies {existing_headline} by addressing {common_hesitation} and promising {measurable_outcome} in {how_long_transformation_takes}. Make it specific and believable.

**Pro tip:** Include a specific timeframe or number. "Triple your confidence" beats "boost your confidence" every time.

## Program Descriptions and Value Propositions

> You are describing a coaching program's curriculum in a way that shows value without overwhelming prospects.
> 
> Program name: {program_title}
> Duration: {length_and_format}
> Main modules: {core_topics_covered}
> Delivery method: {group_calls_or_one_on_one}
> Bonus materials: {additional_resources}
> Support level: {access_to_coach}
> Ideal for: {best_fit_client}
> Not for: {who_shouldnt_join}
> Key outcome: {primary_transformation}
> 
> Write a 200-250 word program description that outlines {main_modules} as benefits, not features. Show how {delivery_method} and {support_level} ensure {key_outcome}. Include what's covered and what's not. End with who this is perfect for and who should wait.

**When to use it:** When prospects ask "what exactly do I get" and you need to show value without creating information overwhelm.

**Pro tip:** Lead each module with the result it creates, then explain how. "Module 1: Stop second-guessing every decision (Decision-making framework)" works better than "Module 1: Decision-making framework."

---

> You are writing a features-to-benefits translation that shows why each program component matters.
> 
> Feature 1: {program_element_1}
> Why it matters: {benefit_to_client_1}
> Feature 2: {program_element_2}
> Why it matters: {benefit_to_client_2}
> Feature 3: {program_element_3}
> Why it matters: {benefit_to_client_3}
> Client type: {target_audience}
> Main struggle: {core_problem_solved}
> 
> Write 3 feature-benefit pairs (40-50 words each) that connect {program_element} to {benefit_to_client}. Focus on what {target_audience} experiences, not what the coach provides. Use "so you can" or "which means" to bridge features to outcomes.

**When to use it:** When your program description reads like a syllabus instead of a transformation roadmap.

**Pro tip:** Start with the benefit, then explain the feature. "Get clarity on your next career move (through our decision matrix tool)" not "Decision matrix tool helps with career clarity."

> You are creating a unique selling proposition that differentiates this coaching program from competitors.
> 
> Coach's background: {relevant_experience}
> Method/framework: {signature_approach}
> Target niche: {specific_audience}
> Common competitor approach: {how_others_do_it}
> Your difference: {what_you_do_instead}
> Proof of concept: {results_or_testimonials}
> Client benefit: {why_difference_matters}
> 
> Write a 80-120 word USP that positions {signature_approach} against {common_competitor_approach}. Show why {your_difference} gets better results for {specific_audience}. Include {proof_of_concept} as credibility. Focus on client outcomes, not coach credentials.

**When to use it:** When prospects are comparison shopping and you need to show why your approach delivers better results than generic coaching.

**Pro tip:** Don't bash competitors. Position your difference as "while most coaches do X, I've found Y works better because..." and let results speak.

**Pro tip:** Use timeline language: "Within 24 hours of enrollment..." and "By week 2, you'll..." to make the experience feel structured and predictable.

## Objection Handling and Risk Reversal

> You are addressing the "I don't have time" objection that busy professionals raise about coaching programs.
> 
> Target audience: {busy_client_description}
> Time commitment: {actual_hours_per_week}
> Current time wasters: {what_they_do_instead}
> Efficiency gains: {how_coaching_saves_time}
> Implementation approach: {bite_sized_or_flexible}
> Success story: {client_who_was_also_busy}
> ROI on time: {what_they_gain_back}
> 
> Write a 100-150 word objection-handling section that acknowledges {busy_client_description} are genuinely pressed for time, then shows how {time_commitment} is less than {current_time_wasters} and delivers {roi_on_time}. Include {success_story} as proof and emphasize {implementation_approach} as doable.

**When to use it:** When your ideal clients are high-achievers who struggle to prioritize their own development despite knowing they need it.

**Pro tip:** Be specific about time requirements. "2 hours per week" is less scary than "ongoing time commitment" and easier to fit into busy schedules.

> You are handling the "I can't afford it" price objection by reframing cost versus investment.
> 
> Program price: {investment_amount}
> Target client income: {approximate_earning_level}
> Cost of status quo: {what_staying_stuck_costs}
> Payment plan: {monthly_breakdown}
> Daily cost: {price_divided_by_program_days}
> Comparable expenses: {what_they_spend_money_on}
> Expected ROI: {financial_or_life_return}
> Alternative solutions: {cost_of_other_approaches}
> 
> Write a 120-180 word response to price objections that breaks down {investment_amount} as {daily_cost} and compares to {comparable_expenses}. Show how {cost_of_status_quo} exceeds the program cost. Mention {payment_plan} and position against {alternative_solutions}. Focus on {expected_roi} not program features.

**When to use it:** When qualified prospects express interest but hesitate at the investment level, especially if they're spending money on less important things.

**Pro tip:** Use their own numbers. If they spend $200/month on things that don't improve their life, show how redirecting that creates transformation.
