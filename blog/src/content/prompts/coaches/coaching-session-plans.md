## Initial Session Planning

> You are a life coach preparing for a discovery session with a new client who's seeking major life change.
> 
> Client name: {client_name}
> Age and background: {age_and_brief_background}
> Primary challenge: {main_issue_they_want_to_address}
> Their stated goal: {what_they_want_to_achieve}
> Session length: {duration_in_minutes}
> Their communication style: {formal / casual / analytical / emotional}
> Previous coaching experience: {none / some / extensive}
> 
> Write a detailed 45-minute session plan using the GROW model. Include 3-4 powerful questions for each stage, specific time allocations, and one breakthrough exercise tailored to their challenge. Format as a session rundown you can reference during the call.

**When to use it:** The night before a first session when you need a structured plan that builds trust and delivers early wins.

**Pro tip:** Always include buffer time between GROW stages. Clients often need longer in the Reality phase when emotions surface unexpectedly.

> You are a life coach designing a follow-up session for a client who's struggling with accountability.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Sessions completed: {number_of_previous_sessions}
> Commitment they missed: {specific_action_they_didnt_complete}
> Their stated reason: {excuse_or_reason_given}
> Pattern you've noticed: {recurring_behavior_or_mindset}
> Your coaching approach: {gentle / direct / solution_focused / exploratory}
> Session focus: {accountability / motivation / obstacle_removal / mindset_shift}
> 
> Create a 60-minute session structure that addresses the missed commitment without judgment. Include a 10-minute check-in, 30-minute exploration using the obstacle-mapping technique, and 20-minute action-planning with built-in accountability measures. End with one homework assignment they can't avoid.

**When to use it:** When a client shows up having not completed their previous commitments and you need to redirect without making them defensive.

**Pro tip:** Lead with curiosity, not consequences. Ask "What did you learn about yourself?" before addressing what they didn't do.

> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Coaching program: {program_name_and_duration}
> Sessions completed: {number} of {total_sessions}
> Major breakthrough: {biggest_win_or_shift_so_far}
> Remaining challenge: {what_theyre_still_working_on}
> Original goal: {what_they_wanted_when_they_started}
> Current energy level: {high / moderate / low / frustrated}
> Next phase focus: {skill_building / deeper_work / maintenance / goal_expansion}
> 
> Design a 75-minute celebration and recalibration session. Start with a 15-minute wins review, spend 30 minutes on progress mapping, 20 minutes identifying what's shifted in their identity, and 10 minutes setting intentions for remaining sessions. Include one reflection exercise that shows them how far they've come.

**When to use it:** Halfway through a coaching package when you need to acknowledge progress and refocus energy for the remaining sessions.

**Pro tip:** Use their own words from session one notes to show the contrast. Nothing motivates like hearing their old limiting beliefs in their own voice.

> You are structuring a crisis coaching session for a client facing an unexpected life disruption.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Crisis situation: {job_loss / relationship_end / health_issue / family_emergency / other}
> How long ago it happened: {timeframe}
> Their emotional state: {overwhelmed / angry / numb / panicked / determined}
> Immediate concern: {what_theyre_most_worried_about_right_now}
> Support system: {strong / limited / absent / complicated}
> Previous coping strategies: {how_theyve_handled_crisis_before}
> Session urgency: {same_day / within_48_hours / this_week}
> 
> Create a 90-minute crisis stabilization session plan. Include 20 minutes for emotional processing, 30 minutes for immediate needs assessment, 25 minutes for short-term action planning, and 15 minutes for follow-up scheduling. Focus on stabilization, not transformation. Include grounding techniques and one emergency coping strategy.

**When to use it:** When a client reaches out in crisis and needs immediate support that's focused on stability rather than growth.

**Pro tip:** Don't try to find the lesson or silver lining in the first crisis session. Focus entirely on helping them feel resourced and capable of handling the next 24-48 hours.

> You are preparing for a goal-setting intensive with a client who's unclear about their direction.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Current life stage: {recent_graduate / career_transition / empty_nester / retirement / other}
> What they know they DON'T want: {clear_things_they_want_to_avoid}
> Values they've mentioned: {top_3_to_5_values_from_previous_conversations}
> Skills they love using: {natural_talents_and_abilities}
> Past goal-setting attempts: {successful / failed / never_tried / started_but_quit}
> Time horizon: {6_months / 1_year / 5_years / unclear}
> Decision-making style: {quick / thorough / collaborative / avoidant}
> 
> Design a 2-hour goal clarification intensive using the values-based goal setting method. Include vision boarding, the regret minimization framework, and backward goal mapping. Structure with 30-minute vision work, 45-minute goal identification, 30-minute obstacle anticipation, and 15-minute first-step commitment. Output should be 3 specific goals with deadlines.

**When to use it:** When a client books a longer session specifically to get clarity on their direction and wants to leave with concrete goals.

**Pro tip:** If they resist choosing goals, ask "What would you regret not trying?" This cuts through perfectionism and analysis paralysis.

## Session Follow-Up Communications

> You are writing a post-session summary email for a life coaching client.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Session focus: {main_topic_or_breakthrough_from_session}
> Key insight they had: {aha_moment_or_realization}
> Commitment they made: {specific_action_or_behavior_change}
> Completion deadline: {when_they_said_theyd_do_it}
> Obstacle they identified: {potential_challenge_they_mentioned}
> Resource mentioned: {book / app / technique / person_you_discussed}
> Next session: {date_and_time}
> Their energy at session end: {excited / determined / uncertain / overwhelmed}
> 
> Write a 250-word follow-up email that reinforces their insight, confirms their commitment, and includes one specific check-in question for mid-week. Include the resource link or next step. Use an encouraging but not cheerleader tone.

**When to use it:** Within 24 hours of every session to increase follow-through and show you were actively listening.

**Pro tip:** Reference something personal they mentioned (like their dog or upcoming family visit) to make it feel human, not templated.

> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Milestone achieved: {specific_accomplishment}
> How long it took: {weeks_or_months_of_work}
> Biggest obstacle they overcame: {main_challenge_that_could_have_stopped_them}
> Old pattern they broke: {previous_behavior_or_mindset_they_changed}
> Who supported them: {family_member_friend_or_colleague_who_helped}
> What this means for their bigger goal: {how_this_connects_to_larger_vision}
> Next challenge coming up: {what_theyre_working_toward_next}
> 
> Write a 150-word celebration message that acknowledges the specific work they did to achieve this, not just the outcome. Reference their growth and what this milestone reveals about their character. End with confidence in their next challenge.

**When to use it:** When a client texts or emails you about completing something significant they've been working toward.

**Pro tip:** Celebrate the process and decision-making they used, not just the result. This builds self-efficacy for future challenges.

> You are writing a gentle accountability check-in for a client who hasn't responded in over a week.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Last session date: {when_you_last_spoke}
> Commitment they made: {what_they_said_theyd_work_on}
> Typical communication pattern: {usually_responsive / sometimes_quiet / often_overwhelmed}
> Life situation: {busy_period / stress / new_relationship / work_demands}
> Your coaching relationship: {new / established / ending_soon}
> Previous no-shows or delays: {none / occasional / pattern}
> Next scheduled session: {date_or_needs_to_be_scheduled}
> 
> Write a 100-word check-in message that opens the door without creating pressure or guilt. Ask one specific question about their week and offer to reschedule if needed. Maintain professional warmth without chasing.

**When to use it:** When a usually communicative client goes quiet and you want to check in without being pushy.

**Pro tip:** Give them an easy out ("I know life gets busy") rather than assuming they're avoiding you. Most silence is about their life, not your coaching.

**Pro tip:** Include a specific visualization technique. "When X happens, see yourself choosing Y" gives them a mental rehearsal tool.

**Pro tip:** Make the prep questions specific to their situation, not generic coaching questions. "How did Tuesday's conversation with your boss shift your confidence?" vs "How was your week?"

## Client Progress Documentation

> You are writing session notes for a breakthrough coaching session.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Session number: {which_session_in_their_program}
> Breakthrough moment: {specific_realization_or_shift_they_had}
> What led to the breakthrough: {question_exercise_or_conversation_that_triggered_it}
> Their emotional response: {tears / excitement / quiet_determination / surprise}
> Old belief they released: {limiting_thought_or_pattern_they_recognized}
> New perspective: {how_theyre_seeing_themselves_or_situation_now}
> Action they committed to: {specific_next_step}
> Follow-up needed: {resource_to_send / check_in_timing / referral_to_make}
> 
> Write detailed 300-word session notes that capture both the content and the emotional journey. Include exact phrases they used for their breakthrough and specific coaching techniques that worked. Format for future session reference.

**When to use it:** Immediately after sessions where significant shifts happened and you need to document what worked.

**Pro tip:** Write breakthrough notes within 30 minutes or you'll lose the exact language they used, which is gold for future sessions.

> Session recovery: {how_you_got_back_on_track / or_if_you_didnt}

> Next session strategy: {different_approach_to_try}

> Write 250-word session notes that objectively document what happened without judgment. Include what you learned about their boundaries and specific approaches to try or avoid in future sessions.

**When to use it:** After sessions where you hit unexpected resistance and need to strategize a different approach.

**Pro tip:** Note their exact words when resistance started. Often there's a phrase that reveals what they're protecting themselves from.
