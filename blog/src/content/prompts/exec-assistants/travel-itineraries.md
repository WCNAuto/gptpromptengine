## Multi-City Business Trip Planning

> You are an executive assistant creating a detailed travel itinerary for a multi-city business trip.
> 
> Executive: {executive_name}
> Trip purpose: {trip_purpose}
> Cities and dates: {city_date_breakdown}
> Flight preferences: {flight_preferences}
> Hotel requirements: {hotel_requirements}
> Key meetings: {meeting_details_with_times}
> Dietary restrictions: {dietary_needs}
> Ground transportation preference: {transport_preference}
> Executive's working style: {morning_person_night_owl_meeting_density}
> 
> Create a comprehensive 3-page travel itinerary organized by day and city. Include flight details with seat preferences, hotel confirmation numbers, meeting locations with travel time buffers, meal recommendations that accommodate dietary needs, and backup contact information. Format with clear timestamps and include a one-page executive summary with key confirmation numbers and emergency contacts.

**When to use it:** When your executive has a complex trip spanning multiple cities with overlapping business objectives and tight scheduling requirements.

**Pro tip:** Always add 30-minute buffers between airport arrival and first meetings—executives hate feeling rushed, and flight delays are common.

## Last-Minute Changes and Urgent Travel

> You are an executive assistant managing same-day flight changes due to meeting schedule conflicts.
> 
> Original flight: {original_flight_details}
> New required arrival: {new_arrival_time_city}
> Meeting conflict: {conflict_description}
> Executive's status: {frequent_flyer_level}
> Baggage situation: {checked_carry_on_none}
> Airline preferences: {preferred_airlines}
> Budget approval: {cost_limit_no_limit}
> Alternative airports: {acceptable_alternatives}
> Ground transportation: {car_service_rental_ride_share}
> 
> Create a same-day flight change solution with multiple options ranked by feasibility. Include rebooking strategies, cost implications, alternative routing through different airports, and ground transportation adjustments. Present as an urgent decision brief with clear recommendations and immediate action steps. Limit to 250-300 words for quick executive review and approval.

**When to use it:** When morning meetings run long and your executive will miss their afternoon flight, requiring immediate rebooking.

**Pro tip:** Call the airline's elite customer service line instead of using the app for same-day changes—phone agents have more flexibility to waive fees and find alternative routings.
