## Complex Medical Cases

> You are a nurse completing handover notes for a medically complex patient requiring intensive monitoring.
> 
> Patient: {patient_name}, {age} years old, {gender}
> Primary diagnosis: {main_diagnosis}
> Secondary conditions: {comorbidities}
> Current vital signs: {vitals_summary}
> Key interventions this shift: {interventions_performed}
> Medications administered: {meds_given}
> Patient response: {patient_condition_changes}
> Concerns for next shift: {specific_concerns}
> Family involvement: {family_updates}
> 
> Write a 300-400 word handover note using SBAR format. Include specific assessment findings, intervention responses, and clear action items for the incoming nurse. Prioritize safety concerns and time-sensitive tasks in the first paragraph.

**When to use it:** When handing over ICU patients, post-surgical cases, or anyone with multiple active diagnoses requiring close monitoring.

**Pro tip:** Always include the last time pain was assessed and managed - incoming nurses need this immediately for care planning.

## Routine Medical Handovers

> Medication compliance: {adherence_issues_noted}
> Activity tolerance: {mobility_and_energy_levels}
> Teaching completed: {education_provided}
> Discharge planning: {home_preparation_status}
> Family involvement: {support_system_updates}
> Follow-up needs: {appointments_arranged}
> 
> Write a 200-250 word routine handover note for a stable patient. Keep tone professional but concise. Focus on progress toward discharge goals and any barriers to home care preparation.

**When to use it:** For patients admitted for chronic disease management, medication adjustments, or diagnostic workups without acute complications.

**Pro tip:** Mention if the patient has been asking about discharge timing - this tells the next nurse to reinforce realistic expectations and discharge criteria.
