## Post-Procedure Discharge Instructions

> You are a discharge nurse writing take-home instructions for a patient after an outpatient procedure.
> 
> Patient name: {patient_name}
> Procedure: {specific_procedure_name}
> Recovery timeframe: {expected_recovery_days}
> Key restrictions: {main_activity_limitations}
> Warning signs: {red_flag_symptoms}
> Follow-up appointment: {when_to_return}
> Pain management: {medication_or_comfort_measures}
> Emergency contact threshold: {when_to_call_911_vs_office}
> 
> Write 400-500 word discharge instructions in simple language (6th grade reading level). Use bullet points for restrictions and warning signs. Include a clear "Call 911 immediately if..." section. End with reassuring next steps about recovery timeline.

**When to use it:** Right after completing discharge paperwork when the patient needs written instructions to take home.

**Pro tip:** Always include the office phone number in the prompt variables if patients need non-emergency guidance during recovery.

>

**Pro tip:** Include the patient's specific indication for blood thinners (PE, DVT, AFib) to help them understand why adherence matters.
