## First Follow-Up Reminders

> You are a freelancer sending a polite first follow-up for an overdue invoice.
> 
> Client name: {client_name}
> Invoice number: {invoice_number}
> Invoice amount: {amount}
> Original due date: {due_date}
> Days overdue: {days_overdue}
> Project completed: {project_name}
> Your relationship length: {relationship_duration}
> Payment terms: {net_15_or_30_days}
> 
> Write a 150 to 200 word email that assumes the delay is an oversight. Open with appreciation for the completed project. Reference the specific invoice details. Include a gentle reminder of payment terms. Close with next steps and maintain a collaborative tone.

**When to use it:** Five to seven days after your payment terms expire, when you want to give the client the benefit of the doubt.

**Pro tip:** Send first follow-ups on Tuesday through Thursday between 10am-2pm for highest open rates with business clients.

## Second Follow-Up Escalation

> You are a freelancer sending a second follow-up for a significantly overdue invoice.
> 
> Client name: {client_name}
> Invoice number: {invoice_number}
> Amount overdue: {invoice_amount}
> Days overdue: {days_overdue}
> First follow-up date: {first_followup_date}
> Project delivered: {deliverable_name}
> Contract payment terms: {payment_terms}
> Late fee policy: {late_fee_amount_or_percentage}
> 
> Write a 200 to 275 word email with increased urgency while remaining professional. Reference your previous follow-up and the lack of response. Mention late fees if applicable per your contract. Set a specific deadline for payment (within 7 days). Indicate next steps if payment isn't received. Maintain respect while showing the seriousness of the situation.

**When to use it:** Two to three weeks after your first follow-up when you've received no response or payment.

**Pro tip:** Always reference your contract terms for late fees rather than introducing new penalties, and be prepared to waive them for immediate payment.

## Final Notice Communications

> You are a freelancer sending a final notice before collections for an extensively overdue invoice.
> 
> Client name: {client_name}
> Invoice number: {invoice_number}
> Amount owed: {total_amount}
> Original due date: {original_due_date}
> Total days overdue: {days_overdue}
> Previous follow-up attempts: {number_of_followups}
> Collections agency: {agency_name_or_process}
> Final deadline: {last_chance_date}
> 
> Write a 250 to 320 word formal final notice email. Reference all previous communication attempts and lack of response. State clearly this is the final notice before collections or legal action. Provide a specific deadline for payment (5-7 business days). Include consequences of non-payment. Maintain professional tone despite firm language. Offer one last opportunity for direct resolution.

**When to use it:** After 60-90 days overdue with multiple failed follow-up attempts, before engaging collections or legal action.

**Pro tip:** Send this via both email and certified mail to create a paper trail for potential legal proceedings.
