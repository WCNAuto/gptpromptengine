## Residential Market Overview Reports

> You are a real estate analyst writing a quarterly residential market overview for investor clients.
> 
> Market area: {neighborhood_or_city}
> Time period: {quarter_year}
> Median home price: {current_median_price}
> Price change from last quarter: {percentage_change}
> Average days on market: {days_on_market}
> Inventory levels: {low_moderate_high}
> Key demographic trend: {population_growth_decline_stable}
> Major economic factor: {employment_rates_new_development_interest_rates}
> Client focus: {first_time_buyers_investors_luxury_market}
> 
> Write a 500-600 word market overview report. Start with a clear market direction statement. Include a "What This Means for Buyers" section and a "What This Means for Sellers" section. End with a 3-month outlook. Use data to support every claim.

**When to use it:** When quarterly numbers just dropped and you have five investor clients expecting market updates by Friday.

**Pro tip:** Replace generic phrases like "the market is strong" with specific metrics. "Inventory down 23% year-over-year" hits harder than "tight supply."

**Pro tip:** Always mention why you excluded certain comparables. "We didn't use 123 Oak Street because it was a foreclosure sale" builds credibility.

> Financing: {down_payment_interest_rate_term}
> Property condition: {move_in_ready_needs_work_major_renovation}
> 
> Write a 450-550 word rental property investment analysis. Calculate cash-on-cash return, cap rate, and total return projections. Include break-even analysis and sensitivity testing for vacancy and repairs. Address property management requirements. End with buy/pass recommendation.

**Pro tip:** Include actual repair estimates, not generic "2% of rent" assumptions. Call contractors for real numbers on HVAC, roofing, and flooring costs.

## Market Trend Analysis

> You are writing a quarterly interest rate impact analysis for real estate clients.
> 
> Current mortgage rates: {thirty_year_fixed_rate}
> Rate change from last quarter: {percentage_point_change}
> Federal Reserve policy: {rate_hike_pause_cut_expectations}
> Buyer demand shift: {application_volume_change}
> Affordability impact: {median_home_payment_change}
> Regional variations: {rates_and_demand_by_metro}
> Refinance activity: {refi_volume_trends}
> Commercial rates: {commercial_lending_rates}
> Economic indicators: {employment_inflation_gdp}
> 
> Write a 500-600 word interest rate impact analysis. Explain rate movements in plain English. Connect interest rate changes to actual buyer behavior and transaction volume. Include regional market variations. Address both residential and commercial implications. End with rate predictions and strategic recommendations.

**When to use it:** When the Fed just announced rate changes and your clients are panicking about market impacts within hours of the news.

**Pro tip:** Include payment examples with real home prices. "A $400k home now costs $200 more per month" resonates more than "rates increased 50 basis points."

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What makes AI prompts effective for real estate market analysis reports?
Effective AI prompts include specific property details, market data variables, and clear output requirements. They produce finished analysis that clients can use immediately, not templates that require additional work.

### How do I customize these prompts for different property types and markets?
Fill in the curly-bracket variables with your specific data—property addresses, market metrics, client requirements, and local conditions. The prompts adapt to residential, commercial, and investment properties across different geographic markets.

### Can these AI-generated reports replace professional real estate analysis?
These prompts create professional draft reports that you should review and customize based on your expertise and local market knowledge. They accelerate analysis creation but don't replace professional judgment and market experience.
