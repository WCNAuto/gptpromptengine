## Property Listing Posts

> You are a real estate agent creating a social media post for a new listing.
> 
> Property type: {single_family_home / condo / townhouse / luxury_estate}
> Address: {street_address_and_neighborhood}
> Price: {listing_price}
> Key features: {three_standout_features}
> Target buyer: {first_time_buyers / families / investors / luxury_buyers}
> Platform: {instagram / facebook / linkedin}
> Urgency factor: {just_listed / price_reduced / open_house_this_weekend}
> 
> Write a 120-150 word social media caption that opens with a hook question, highlights the three key features with benefit-focused language, includes the price and neighborhood, and ends with a clear call-to-action. Use 3-5 relevant hashtags.

**When to use it:** Thursday afternoons when you're uploading fresh listings before the weekend rush.

**Pro tip:** If the property has been on the market over 30 days, use "price_reduced" or focus on a recent improvement rather than "just_listed" to maintain momentum.

> You are announcing a price reduction with strategic positioning.
> 
> Original price: {original_listing_price}
> New price: {reduced_price}
> Reduction amount: {dollar_amount_or_percentage}
> Time on market: {weeks_or_months}
> Market shift reason: {interest_rates / seasonal / inventory_increase / appraisal_reality}
> Property strength: {location / condition / features / potential}
> Urgency creator: {motivated_seller / relocation / investment_opportunity}
> 
> Write a 140-180 word post that frames the price reduction as a market opportunity rather than desperation. Open with the new price and savings, explain the market context briefly, highlight why the property is still a strong value, and create urgency around the motivated timeline.

**When to use it:** Monday mornings after a weekend with low showing activity, to restart momentum for the week.

**Pro tip:** Post the price reduction simultaneously across all platforms within a 30-minute window to maximize the "breaking news" effect and reach.

## Market Update Content

> You are sharing monthly market statistics with your local expertise.
> 
> Month/Year: {specific_month_and_year}
> Key metric change: {median_price_up_down / inventory_change / days_on_market}
> Percentage change: {specific_percentage}
> Local market area: {city_neighborhood_or_county}
> Interest rate context: {current_rate_and_trend}
> Your prediction: {optimistic / cautious / stable / opportunity}
> Audience: {buyers / sellers / investors / general}
> 
> Write a 180-220 word market update that opens with the most surprising statistic, explains what it means for your audience in practical terms, connects it to interest rate impacts, and ends with specific advice for buyers or sellers this month.

**When to use it:** First Tuesday of every month when new market data becomes available from your MLS.

**Pro tip:** Save your market update as a highlight reel on Instagram Stories – people reference these throughout the month when making real estate decisions.

## Client Success Stories

> You are sharing a client success story that highlights your problem-solving skills.
> 
> Client type: {first_time_buyer / seller / investor / relocating_family}
> Challenge faced: {low_inventory / bidding_wars / appraisal_gap / timing_crunch}
> Your solution: {creative_offer / off_market_find / negotiation_strategy / vendor_network}
> Outcome: {got_the_house / sold_above_asking / closed_on_time / saved_money}
> Timeline: {days_weeks_or_months}
> Client emotion: {relieved / excited / grateful / surprised}
> Lesson for others: {specific_advice_or_insight}
> 
> Write a 120-150 word client story that focuses more on your problem-solving than the client's personal details, shows your expertise in action, and ends with practical advice other clients can apply.

**When to use it:** Within one week of closing, while the success and gratitude are fresh.

**Pro tip:** Ask clients for permission to share their story at the final walkthrough – they're most excited then and usually happy to help you help others.
