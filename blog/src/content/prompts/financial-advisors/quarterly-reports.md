## Portfolio Performance Summaries

> You are a financial advisor writing the executive summary section of a quarterly investment report.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Portfolio value start of quarter: {starting_value}
> Portfolio value end of quarter: {ending_value}
> Benchmark performance: {benchmark_return_percentage}
> Portfolio return: {portfolio_return_percentage}
> Asset allocation: {current_allocation_breakdown}
> Major position changes: {two_to_three_key_trades}
> Market environment: {bullish / bearish / volatile / stable}
> 
> Write a 300 to 400 word executive summary that opens with the bottom-line performance number, explains how we performed versus the benchmark, highlights the two most impactful portfolio decisions this quarter, and sets context for the market environment. Use confident but measured language. Include one forward-looking sentence about positioning for next quarter.

**When to use it:** When you need to summarize complex portfolio movements for clients who want the big picture first.

**Pro tip:** Always lead with the absolute dollar change before percentages - clients care more about "$50,000 gain" than "8.2% return."

> You are a financial advisor explaining underperformance in a quarterly report.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Portfolio return: {portfolio_return_percentage}
> Benchmark return: {benchmark_return_percentage}
> Underperformance amount: {negative_difference}
> Main detractor: {worst_performing_position}
> Detractor impact: {dollar_amount_lost}
> Strategic rationale: {why_we_held_this_position}
> Action taken: {sold / reduced / held}
> Offsetting winner: {best_performing_position}
> 
> Write a 200 to 250 word explanation that acknowledges the underperformance directly, explains the strategic reasoning behind the detractor position, states the corrective action taken, and reinforces confidence in the overall strategy. Use accountability language, not market blame.

**When to use it:** When your portfolio lagged the benchmark and you need to explain the gap without sounding defensive.

**Pro tip:** Mention the specific dollar impact of the detractor - it shows you're tracking exact costs of your decisions.

> You are a financial advisor addressing concentrated position risk in a quarterly report.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Concentrated position: {company_or_sector_name}
> Position size: {percentage_of_portfolio}
> Performance this quarter: {return_percentage}
> Reason for concentration: {inheritance / employer_stock / conviction_bet}
> Risk assessment: {high / moderate / manageable}
> Diversification plan: {reduce_gradually / hedge / hold}
> Timeline: {next_quarter / six_months / one_year}
> Tax considerations: {significant / minimal / none}
> 
> Write a 300 to 350 word risk management discussion that acknowledges the concentration, explains why it exists, quantifies the portfolio risk, outlines the plan to manage it, and addresses any tax implications of changes. Balanced tone that respects client attachment while highlighting professional concerns.

**When to use it:** When a single holding represents more than 15% of a client's portfolio and you need to address the elephant in the room.

**Pro tip:** Frame concentration risk in terms of "what happens if this position drops 20%" rather than abstract risk statistics.

## Market Commentary and Outlook

> You are a financial advisor writing market commentary for a quarterly investment report.
> 
> Quarter: {Q1 / Q2 / Q3 / Q4} {year}
> Major market events: {two_to_three_key_events}
> Interest rate environment: {rising / falling / stable}
> Inflation trend: {accelerating / decelerating / stable}
> Economic indicators: {strong / mixed / weak}
> Sector rotation: {growth_to_value / value_to_growth / defensive / cyclical}
> Geopolitical factors: {specific_events_or_none}
> Volatility level: {high / moderate / low}
> 
> Write a 400 to 450 word market commentary that summarizes the quarter's key themes, explains how different asset classes performed and why, identifies the main drivers of market movements, and avoids predictions while setting context for portfolio positioning. Professional but accessible language.

**When to use it:** When clients ask "what happened in the markets this quarter" and you need comprehensive but digestible context.

**Pro tip:** Link each market trend to a specific impact on the client's portfolio - don't let commentary float unconnected to their holdings.

> You are a financial advisor explaining sector rotation in a quarterly report.
> 
> Winning sectors: {three_top_performing_sectors}
> Losing sectors: {three_worst_performing_sectors}
> Rotation driver: {interest_rates / economic_cycle / earnings / sentiment}
> Portfolio positioning: {overweight / underweight / neutral} to winners
> Position changes made: {specific_sector_trades}
> Forward outlook: {rotation_continues / reversal_expected / unclear}
> Client portfolio impact: {positive / negative / neutral}
> Next quarter strategy: {follow_trend / contrarian / maintain}
> 
> Write a 250 to 300 word sector analysis that explains which sectors moved and why, describes how your portfolio was positioned relative to these moves, quantifies the impact on client returns, and indicates your strategy for the coming quarter. Focus on cause and effect relationships.

**When to use it:** After quarters with significant sector rotation when you need to explain performance relative to style benchmarks.

**Pro tip:** If you missed a sector rotation, explain the risk management logic that kept you underweight rather than apologizing for missing the move.

## Asset Allocation and Strategy Updates

> You are a financial advisor explaining a tactical asset allocation change in a quarterly report.
> 
> Previous allocation: {old_stock_bond_cash_percentages}
> New allocation: {new_stock_bond_cash_percentages}
> Change driver: {market_conditions / valuation / economic_data}
> Timing of change: {early / mid / late_quarter}
> Performance impact: {positive / negative / neutral}
> Risk level change: {increased / decreased / maintained}
> Duration of tactical shift: {temporary / medium_term / strategic}
> Reversal trigger: {specific_conditions_for_change_back}
> 
> Write a 275 to 325 word allocation update that explains exactly what changed and when, provides the strategic rationale behind the timing, quantifies the impact on portfolio risk and return, and indicates what conditions would trigger a reversal. Clear decision-making framework.

**When to use it:** After making significant allocation changes that deviated from the client's strategic targets.

**Pro tip:** State the specific trigger that caused the allocation change so clients understand your decision-making process.

> You are a financial advisor justifying a defensive portfolio positioning in a quarterly report.
> 
> Current equity allocation: {percentage_below_target}
> Cash/short-term allocation: {higher_than_normal_percentage}
> Defensive rationale: {market_valuation / economic_uncertainty / technical_indicators}
> Opportunity cost: {return_given_up_by_being_defensive}
> Risk avoided: {potential_downside_protection}
> Redeployment plan: {specific_conditions_to_add_risk}
> Timeline: {weeks / months / quarters}
> Performance vs staying fully invested: {better / worse / similar}
> 
> Write a 300 to 350 word defensive positioning explanation that acknowledges the opportunity cost of being conservative, explains the specific risks being avoided, quantifies the protection provided, and outlines clear conditions for returning to normal risk levels. Confident but humble tone.

**When to use it:** When you've reduced risk and clients question missing out on market gains.

**Pro tip:** Quantify both the returns given up and the downside avoided to show the full risk-return tradeoff of defensive positioning.
