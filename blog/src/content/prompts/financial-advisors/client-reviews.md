## Annual Portfolio Reviews

> You are a Financial Advisor writing an annual portfolio review for a long-term client.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Portfolio value: {current_value}
> Year-over-year change: {percentage_change}
> Major holdings performance: {top_three_performers_and_returns}
> Market context: {key_market_events_this_year}
> Client risk tolerance: {conservative / moderate / aggressive}
> Upcoming life events: {retirement_timeline_or_major_expenses}
> 
> Write a 450 to 550 word annual review that opens with the portfolio's overall performance in context of market conditions. Detail the top three holdings and their contribution to returns. Address how the portfolio weathered market volatility relative to the client's risk tolerance. Close with three specific recommendations for the coming year tied to their life stage and goals.

**When to use it:** During your annual review season when you're writing comprehensive performance summaries for established clients.

**Pro tip:** Always mention specific market events (Fed rate changes, sector rotations) to show you're actively managing around external factors, not just riding the market.

> You are a Financial Advisor writing a year-end review for a client who experienced significant portfolio losses.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Portfolio decline: {percentage_loss}
> Original investment timeline: {years_until_goal}
> Specific underperformers: {worst_performing_holdings}
> Market benchmark comparison: {how_portfolio_performed_vs_sp500}
> Client emotional state: {anxious / frustrated / understanding}
> Recovery strategy: {rebalancing_approach}
> 
> Write a 400 to 500 word review that acknowledges the loss directly in the opening. Provide context by comparing performance to relevant benchmarks and explaining specific factors that drove underperformance. Present a clear recovery timeline and strategy. End with reassurance grounded in historical market data and the client's long-term timeline.

**When to use it:** After a difficult market year when you need to address losses while maintaining client confidence and preventing panic selling.

**Pro tip:** Include specific historical recovery timelines (like "the average bear market recovery takes 14 months") to give anxious clients concrete expectations rather than vague reassurances.

## Quarterly Progress Reports

> You are a Financial Advisor writing a quarterly progress report after a strong market quarter.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Quarterly return: {percentage_gain}
> Year-to-date performance: {ytd_return}
> Best performing holding: {top_performer_and_gain}
> Benchmark comparison: {vs_relevant_index}
> Rebalancing actions: {any_profit_taking_or_adjustments}
> Market outlook: {next_quarter_expectations}
> 
> Write a 250 to 350 word quarterly update that celebrates the strong performance while managing expectations for sustainability. Highlight the best-performing holding and explain why it succeeded. Address any rebalancing moves made to lock in gains. Provide realistic expectations for the next quarter based on current market conditions.

**When to use it:** After a particularly strong quarter when you want to acknowledge success while preventing overconfidence or unrealistic future expectations.

**Pro tip:** Always mention what you sold or trimmed during strong periods to show active management—clients need to see you're taking profits, not just riding momentum.

> You are a Financial Advisor writing a quarterly report during market volatility.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Quarterly portfolio movement: {percentage_change}
> Volatility level experienced: {standard_deviation_or_description}
> Defensive actions taken: {any_hedging_or_cash_raising}
> Opportunity purchases: {any_buying_during_dips}
> Client stress level: {calling_frequency_or_concern_level}
> Historical context: {similar_periods_in_past}
> 
> Write a 300 to 400 word quarterly report that normalizes market volatility as expected behavior. Reference specific historical periods with similar volatility and their eventual outcomes. Detail any tactical moves made to protect capital or capitalize on opportunities. Reassure without dismissing legitimate concerns about market conditions.

**When to use it:** During high-volatility quarters when clients are experiencing daily portfolio swings and need steady guidance.

**Pro tip:** Include specific volatility metrics (like "daily moves over 1% occurred 12 times this quarter vs. 4 times normally") to help clients understand what they're experiencing is measurable and temporary.

## Client Goal Progress Updates

> You are a Financial Advisor tracking progress toward a specific savings goal.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Savings goal: {house_down_payment / education / other_specific_target}
> Target amount: {dollar_goal}
> Target timeline: {months_or_years_remaining}
> Current progress: {amount_saved_toward_goal}
> Monthly contribution: {regular_savings_amount}
> Investment performance impact: {how_returns_affected_progress}
> Projected completion: {on_track / ahead / behind_schedule}
> 
> Write a 250 to 350 word progress update that clearly shows percentage toward goal completion. Calculate whether current savings rate will meet the timeline. Address how investment performance has helped or hindered progress. Recommend adjustments to contributions or timeline if needed to stay on track.

**When to use it:** When reviewing progress on specific savings goals like home down payments, education funding, or major purchases.

**Pro tip:** Always provide multiple scenarios—if they're behind, show both "increase contributions by X" and "extend timeline by Y months" options so clients can choose what works.

> 
> Write a 400 to 500 word retirement readiness assessment that projects current savings trajectory. Compare projected retirement income to desired lifestyle costs. **Pro tip:** Use real numbers, not percentages—"you'll have $47,000 annual income but want $75,000" is more motivating than "you're 37% behind your goal."
