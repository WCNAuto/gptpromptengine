## Executive Summary Reports

> You are a Customer Success Director writing an executive churn risk summary for the leadership team.
> 
> Analysis period: {quarter_and_year}
> Overall churn risk level: {low/medium/high}
> Customers analyzed: {total_customer_count}
> High-risk accounts: {number_at_risk}
> Revenue at risk: {dollar_amount}
> Primary risk drivers: {top_three_causes}
> Mitigation actions taken: {current_interventions}
> 
> Write a 400-500 word executive summary that opens with the headline risk number and revenue impact. Use bullet points for the top 3 risk segments. Include a decisive recommendation for leadership action in the final paragraph. Keep the tone data-driven and urgent without being alarmist.

**When to use it:** Before monthly or quarterly business reviews when leadership needs churn exposure numbers fast.

**Pro tip:** Lead with the revenue number, not the percentage. "$2.3M at risk" hits harder than "15% churn risk" in executive meetings.

## Individual Account Risk Assessments

> You are a Customer Success Manager writing a high-priority churn risk assessment for an enterprise account.
> 
> Customer: {company_name}
> Annual contract value: {arr_amount}
> Renewal date: {month_day_year}
> Primary risk indicators: {usage_drops_support_tickets_complaints}
> Stakeholder changes: {new_contacts_or_departures}
> Business impact: {how_customer_business_has_changed}
> Competitive activity: {competitor_meetings_or_evaluations}
> Relationship strength: {champion_strength_and_detractor_presence}
> Mitigation steps taken: {actions_completed_in_last_30_days}
> Executive sponsor needed: {yes_no_and_level_required}
> 
> Write a 350-400 word individual account risk assessment that reads like an urgent action plan. Start with renewal probability percentage. Use bullet points for risk factors and mitigation steps. End with clear next actions and timeline. Keep the tone clinical and decisive.

**When to use it:** When flagging individual high-value accounts for leadership intervention.

**Pro tip:** Include competitor intelligence even if it's limited. "No competitive activity detected" is data too.

>

> Total accounts in segment: {number_of_customers}
> Churn rate trend: {percentage_and_direction}
> Common risk factors: {top_three_reasons_smbs_churn}
> Price sensitivity: {how_cost_affects_retention}
> Support requirements: {self_service_vs_high_touch_needs}
> Product fit challenges: {features_too_complex_or_too_simple}
> Competitive alternatives: {cheaper_or_simpler_solutions}
> Success patterns: {what_makes_smbs_sticky}
> Recommended interventions: {specific_retention_strategies_for_smbs}
> 
> Write a 450-500 word SMB segment churn analysis that addresses scale challenges and resource constraints. Focus on systemic issues, not individual accounts. Include recommendations for automated interventions and self-service improvements. Use data to support retention investment decisions.

**When to use it:** During quarterly business reviews when analyzing churn patterns across customer size segments.

**Pro tip:** SMB churn is often about onboarding and early value realization. Focus retention efforts on the first 90 days.
