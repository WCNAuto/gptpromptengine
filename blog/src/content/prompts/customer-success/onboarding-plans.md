## New Enterprise Customer Onboarding Plans

> You are a Customer Success Manager creating a 90-day onboarding plan for a new enterprise customer.
> 
> Customer: {company_name}
> Industry: {industry}
> Contract value: {annual_contract_value}
> Primary use case: {main_business_problem_being_solved}
> Key stakeholders: {decision_maker_name_and_title}, {technical_contact_name_and_title}, {end_user_champion_name_and_title}
> Implementation complexity: {low / medium / high}
> Success metrics: {primary_kpi}, {secondary_kpi}
> Previous tool they're replacing: {competitor_or_manual_process}
> Go-live date: {target_date}
> 
> Write a detailed 90-day onboarding plan structured in 30-60-90 day phases. Include specific milestones, deliverables, and success criteria for each phase. Format as a plan you can send to the customer and internal team. 600 to 800 words.

**When to use it:** Right after contract signature when you need to align internal teams and set customer expectations before the kickoff call.

**Pro tip:** Always include buffer time in your dates. Enterprise implementations take 20% longer than planned, so build that cushion into your timeline upfront.

## Technical Integration Onboarding

> You are creating an onboarding plan focused on complex technical integration.
> 
> Customer: {company_name}
> Integration type: {api_crm_database_or_other}
> Technical contact: {developer_or_it_contact_name}
> Systems involved: {existing_tools_to_integrate}
> Data migration: {volume_and_complexity}
> Custom requirements: {specific_technical_needs}
> Development timeline: {technical_work_estimate}
> Testing approach: {qa_and_validation_process}
> Rollback plan: {contingency_if_integration_fails}
> 
> Write a technical onboarding plan with parallel workstreams for business setup and technical integration. Include testing milestones and fallback options. Structure with clear technical and business tracks. 500 to 600 words.

**When to use it:** When the customer's success depends heavily on technical integration rather than just configuration.

**Pro tip:** Always plan a soft launch period where integration runs parallel to old systems. Never switch everything over on day one, no matter how confident your technical team feels.

> User count: {total_number_of_end_users}

> Training preference: {in_person_virtual_or_self_paced}
> Change readiness: {team_enthusiasm_level}
> Previous tool: {what_users_are_familiar_with}
> Power users: {names_of_potential_champions}
> Training timeline: {when_training_must_be_complete}
> Success metrics: {adoption_goals}
> 
> Write a user adoption onboarding plan with role-based training tracks and champion development. Include practice exercises and competency validation. Structure as a training curriculum with clear learning objectives. 500 to 600 words.

**When to use it:** When user adoption is the primary success factor and technical setup is straightforward.

**Pro tip:** Identify and train your power users two weeks before general training begins. They become your unofficial support team during the broader rollout.
