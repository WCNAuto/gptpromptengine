## Meeting Rescheduling and Conflicts

> You are a virtual assistant writing an email to reschedule a meeting due to a conflict.
> 
> Client: {client_name}
> Meeting type: {meeting_type}
> Original date/time: {original_datetime}
> Reason for change: {conflict_reason}
> New proposed times: {three_alternative_slots}
> Urgency level: {low / medium / high}
> Relationship tone: {formal / professional / friendly}
> Notice period: {same_day / 24_hours / week_ahead}
> 
> Write a 150-200 word email that apologizes for the change, briefly explains the conflict, offers three specific alternative times, and requests confirmation. Use a subject line that clearly indicates rescheduling. End with a direct call-to-action for their preferred time.

**When to use it:** When a double-booking or urgent client matter forces you to move an existing appointment within 48 hours.

**Pro tip:** Always lead with the apology and new options before explaining the reason. Clients want solutions first, context second.

>

> Other attendees: {attendee_list_with_roles}

> Duration needed: {meeting_length}
> Timezone considerations: {timezone_challenges}
> Availability windows: {limited_time_slots}

> Deadline for scheduling: {scheduling_deadline}
> 
> Write a 250-300 word coordination email that presents 2-3 time options that work for the majority, acknowledges the scheduling complexity, and establishes a clear decision timeline. Include a brief agenda outline to justify the meeting length and attendee list.

**When to use it:** When organizing cross-team or client meetings where 4+ people have conflicting availability across different time zones.

**Pro tip:** Always identify the decision maker upfront and give them 24-48 hours to confirm, with automatic default selection if no response.

> Core attendees: {regular_participants}

> Meeting length: {time_duration}
> Agenda structure: {meeting_format}

> 
> Write a 280-320 word series launch email that establishes the meeting's value proposition, sets clear expectations for preparation and participation, creates accountability for attendance, and includes a built-in evaluation mechanism to prevent meeting bloat.

**When to use it:** When launching new recurring meetings that need strong adoption and sustained value delivery.

**Pro tip:** Set a 90-day review date in the initial launch to evaluate effectiveness and prevent the meeting from becoming a permanent calendar fixture without ongoing value.
