## Weekly Progress Summaries

> You are a Virtual Assistant writing a weekly progress report for a client.
> 
> Client name: {client_name}
> Report period: {start_date} to {end_date}
> Main project focus: {primary_project}
> Tasks completed this week:
> {completed_tasks_in_bullets}
> Tasks in progress:
> {ongoing_tasks_with_status}
> Blockers or delays:
> {issues_or_none}
> Hours worked: {total_hours}
> Client tone preference: {professional / casual / detailed}
> 
> Write a 300 to 400 word weekly progress report. Start with a brief overview of the week's focus. List completed tasks with specific outcomes. Address any blockers directly with proposed solutions. End with next week's priorities and expected deliverables.

**When to use it:** Every Friday afternoon before logging off, when you need to update clients on weekly progress without spending 30 minutes crafting the email.

**Pro tip:** If you hit a major blocker, mention it in the middle section but always pair it with your proposed solution or alternative approach.

## Project Milestone Reports

> You are a Virtual Assistant reporting completion of a major project phase to a client.
> 
> Client name: {client_name}
> Project: {project_name}
> Phase completed: {milestone_name}
> Completion date: {milestone_date}
> Deliverables in this phase:
> {phase_deliverables}
> Quality checks passed: {testing_or_review_completed}
> Next phase: {upcoming_milestone}
> Dependencies resolved: {blockers_cleared}
> Budget impact: {hours_used_vs_planned}
> 
> Write a 300 to 350 word milestone completion report. Confirm what was delivered, highlight any quality assurance completed, identify what's needed to start the next phase, and provide a clear timeline for upcoming work.

**When to use it:** Immediately after completing any major project milestone that represents 20% or more of the total project scope.

**Pro tip:** Always include what you need from the client to begin the next phase - approval, access, content, or decisions.

**Pro tip:** Always provide at least two viable options - clients need to feel they're making a choice, not just approving your only idea.

## Client Communication Updates

> You are a Virtual Assistant providing a quick daily standup-style update to a client who prefers frequent brief communication.
> 
> Client name: {client_name}
> Date: {update_date}
> Yesterday completed: {tasks_finished_yesterday}
> Today working on: {current_day_focus}
> Tomorrow planned: {next_day_priorities}
> Blockers: {obstacles_or_none}
> Quick wins: {small_successes_to_highlight}
> Questions for client: {decisions_or_input_needed}
> 
> Write a 100 to 150 word daily standup update in bullet format. Keep it scannable, action-oriented, and end with any questions that need quick client responses.

**When to use it:** For clients who prefer daily touchpoints, usually sent first thing in the morning or end of day.

**Pro tip:** Keep the same structure every day - clients will scan for their section (usually "Questions for client") and appreciate the consistency.
