## Standard Role Offers

> You are a recruiter writing a formal job offer letter for a standard hire.
> 
> Candidate: {candidate_name}
> Role: {job_title}
> Department: {department}
> Start date: {start_date}
> Salary: {annual_salary}
> Manager: {manager_name}
> Company: {company_name}
> Key benefits: {top_three_benefits}
> Response deadline: {response_by_date}
> 
> Write a 250-300 word professional offer letter. Open with congratulations and excitement. Include salary, start date, reporting structure, and benefits in the middle. Close with next steps and response timeline. Use formal but warm tone.

**When to use it:** When you've completed final interviews for mid-level roles and need to send the offer within 24 hours of decision.

**Pro tip:** Always include the exact response deadline date, not "within one week" - it creates urgency and prevents confusion about weekends.

**Pro tip:** Include equity vesting schedule details or reference a separate equity agreement - executives want specifics on their largest compensation component.

## Urgent and Competitive Offers

> You are a recruiter writing an urgent offer letter to secure a candidate who has competing offers.
> 
> Candidate: {candidate_name}
> Role: {job_title}
> Our salary: {competitive_salary}
> Unique advantages: {top_three_differentiators}
> Decision timeline: {short_response_deadline}
> Signing bonus: {immediate_signing_bonus}
> Growth opportunity: {specific_growth_path}
> Team/culture fit: {team_culture_highlights}
> Start date: {flexible_start_date}
> Manager: {manager_name}
> 
> Write a 250-300 word compelling offer letter that stands out from competitors. Lead with unique advantages. Address urgency professionally. Use confident language that shows you really want them without appearing desperate.

**When to use it:** When you know the candidate has other offers and you need to differentiate quickly without starting a bidding war.

**Pro tip:** Lead with non-monetary differentiators first - if you open with salary, you've started a bidding war you might not win.
