## Cold Outreach Messages

> You are a recruiter reaching out to a passive candidate on LinkedIn.
> 
> Candidate name: {candidate_name}
> Current company: {current_company}
> Current role: {current_role}
> Target role: {target_role}
> Your company: {your_company}
> Specific skill/achievement you noticed: {specific_skill_or_achievement}
> Meeting format: {phone_call / coffee / video_chat}
> Your tone: {casual / professional / direct}
> Industry: {industry}
> 
> Write a 120-150 word LinkedIn InMail message. Start with a specific compliment about their work or achievement. Explain the opportunity without overselling. End with a low-pressure meeting request. Use their name once. No buzzwords like "rockstar" or "ninja."

**When to use it:** When you've found a strong passive candidate but need to break through the noise of generic recruiter messages.

**Pro tip:** Reference a recent post they made or a project their company announced. It proves you're not mass-messaging and increases response rates by 40%.

> 
> Candidate name: {candidate_name}
> Original role mentioned: {original_role}
> Days since first message: {days_since_first_message}
> New information to share: {new_information}
> Alternative role option: {alternative_role_option}
> Your company: {your_company}
> Urgency level: {low / medium / high}
> Tone: {understanding / persistent / casual}
> 
> Write a 90-110 word follow-up LinkedIn message. Acknowledge they're probably busy. Share one new piece of information or an alternative opportunity. Give them an easy out while keeping the door open. Don't repeat the original pitch.

**When to use it:** When a promising candidate has gone quiet after 5-7 days but you know they saw your message.

**Pro tip:** If they looked at your profile after your message, mention a mutual connection or shared background detail from your profile to create a warmer follow-up.

## Boolean Search Strings

> You are creating a LinkedIn boolean search string for a technical role.
> 
> Target role: {target_role}
> Required skills (3-5): {required_skills}
> Nice-to-have skills (2-3): {nice_to_have_skills}
> Years of experience: {experience_range}
> Industry focus: {industry_or_any}
> Location: {location}
> Exclude companies: {companies_to_exclude}
> Include companies: {target_companies}
> Education requirements: {education_or_none}
> 
> Write a LinkedIn boolean search string using proper operators (AND, OR, NOT, quotes, parentheses). Prioritize required skills with AND operators. Group similar skills with OR. Exclude irrelevant titles and industries. Format it as a single line ready to copy into LinkedIn's search bar.

**When to use it:** When you need a precise search string for technical roles where skills matter more than job titles.

**Pro tip:** Test your boolean string in LinkedIn's regular search first, then refine it in Sales Navigator. The syntax works differently and regular search shows you if your logic is broken.

## Candidate Research Briefs

> You are researching a candidate before an initial recruiting call.
> 
> Candidate name: {candidate_name}
> LinkedIn profile URL: {linkedin_url}
> Current role: {current_role}
> Current company: {current_company}
> Target role: {target_role}
> Key skills to assess: {skills_to_validate}
> Career progression pattern: {career_progression}
> Education background: {education}
> Recent activities/posts: {recent_linkedin_activity}
> 
> Write a 200-250 word research brief for the recruiting team. Include career highlights, potential concerns, conversation starters, and 3-4 specific questions to ask. Focus on gaps to explore and strengths to confirm. End with a recommended talk track approach.

**When to use it:** Before your first phone screen when you need to quickly understand a candidate's background and prepare targeted questions.

**Pro tip:** Check their recommendations and endorsements section. Former colleagues' comments often reveal working style and real strengths better than the resume.

> Role they're interviewing for: {target_role}
> Hiring manager: {hiring_manager_name}

> Cultural fit indicators: {cultural_fit_signals}
> Compensation expectations: {comp_expectations}
> Interview stage: {phone_screen / onsite / final}
> Timeline pressure: {high / medium / low}
> 
> Write a 150-200 word hiring manager brief. Lead with why this candidate is worth their time. Flag any concerns upfront with suggested probing questions. Include 2-3 conversation starters. End with next steps and timing recommendations.

**When to use it:** When you need to quickly prep a busy hiring manager before they interview a candidate you've screened.

**Pro tip:** Include one personal detail (hobby, volunteer work, shared alma mater) that the hiring manager can use to build rapport in the first few minutes.
