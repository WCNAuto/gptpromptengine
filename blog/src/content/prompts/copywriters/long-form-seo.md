## Product-Led SEO Articles

> You are a copywriter creating a comprehensive product comparison article for SEO.
> 
> Target keyword: {primary_keyword}
> Product 1: {product_one_name}
> Product 2: {product_two_name}
> Product 3: {product_three_name}
> Audience pain point: {specific_problem_they_solve}
> Price range: {budget_low} to {budget_high}
> Key differentiator: {main_feature_difference}
> Brand tone: {professional / conversational / authoritative}
> Article length: {1500 / 2000 / 2500} words
> 
> Write a long-form SEO article comparing these three products. Open with a hook addressing the pain point and promise a clear winner by the end. Use H2 subheadings for each product, H3s for features, pricing, pros/cons. Include a comparison table with 5-7 key specs. End with a definitive recommendation based on use case, not a cop-out "it depends" conclusion.

**When to use it:** When you need to rank for "[product type] comparison" keywords and have three competing solutions to evaluate.

**Pro tip:** Include actual pricing and feature details in your variables. Vague comparisons don't convert or rank well in 2026's AI-heavy search landscape.

> You are a copywriter writing a product review article targeting high-intent buyers.
> 
> Product name: {product_name}
> Target keyword: {primary_seo_keyword}
> Price point: {exact_price}
> Main competitor: {competitor_name}
> Unique selling proposition: {key_differentiator}
> Target audience: {specific_user_type}
> Personal experience: {your_usage_timeframe}
> Biggest drawback: {honest_limitation}
> Brand voice: {helpful / skeptical / enthusiastic}
> 
> Write a 1800-word honest product review. Start with your verdict upfront, then justify it. Include sections: What it does, who it's for, hands-on experience, pricing breakdown, vs {competitor_name}, final verdict. Use first-person throughout. Be specific about the drawback - readers trust honest reviews. Include 3-5 H2 sections with descriptive subheadings that include secondary keywords.

**When to use it:** When targeting "[product name] review" keywords or writing affiliate content that needs to rank and convert.

**Pro tip:** Put your conclusion first, not last. High-intent buyers want the verdict immediately, then scan for supporting details.

> You are writing a "best of" listicle for copywriters targeting competitive SEO keywords.
> 
> Main keyword: {target_keyword_phrase}
> List size: {number_of_items}
> Category: {product_or_service_type}
> Audience level: {beginner / intermediate / advanced}
> Budget consideration: {free / budget / premium / mixed}
> Primary use case: {specific_job_to_be_done}
> Your top pick: {recommended_option}
> Ranking criteria: {what_makes_something_best}
> Tone: {authoritative / friendly / no_nonsense}
> 
> Write a 2200-word "best of" article. Open with why this list exists and your ranking methodology. Give each item 150-200 words: what it does, why it made the list, who should use it, one specific pro and con. Use H2s for each item titled "X. [Product Name]: Best for [Specific Use Case]". Include a quick-reference comparison table after the intro. End with a clear winner for the most common use case.

**When to use it:** When targeting "best [category] for [use case]" keywords that drive high-converting organic traffic.

**Pro tip:** Make your ranking criteria specific and defensible. "Best overall" means nothing. "Best for teams under 10 people" or "best for beginners with no coding experience" ranks better and converts harder.

> 
> Industry trend: {current_development_or_change}
> Your stance: {contrarian_view_or_unique_angle}

> Predicted outcome: {what_you_think_will_happen}
> Call to action: {what_readers_should_do}
> Voice: {provocative / measured / visionary}
> 
> Write a 1400-word thought leadership article. Open with a bold statement about {industry_trend} that challenges conventional wisdom. Present your {contrarian_view} with {supporting_evidence}. Address obvious counterarguments in a dedicated section. Paint a picture of what happens if the industry ignores this trend vs embraces it. **When to use it:** When your client needs to establish authority in their space and you're targeting industry-specific keywords with commercial intent.

**Pro tip:** Take a clear stance and defend it with specifics. Wishy-washy thought leadership doesn't get shared or remembered.
