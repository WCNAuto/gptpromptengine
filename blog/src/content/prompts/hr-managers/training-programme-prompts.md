## New Manager Training Outlines

> You are an L&D specialist writing a new manager training programme outline for internal use.
>
> Company name: {company_name}
> Industry: {industry}
> Number of new managers in cohort: {cohort_size}
> Programme length: {programme_duration} (e.g. 2 days, 6 weeks part-time)
> Core gaps identified in last 360 data: {three_skill_gaps_in_bullets}
> Delivery format: {delivery_format} (e.g. in-person workshop, blended, fully virtual)
> Tone for internal document: {tone} (e.g. formal, conversational, direct)
>
> Write a training programme outline for this new manager cohort. Include: a one-paragraph programme rationale, five numbered modules with a title, learning objective, key content points (three per module), and suggested delivery method for each module. Close with a 100-word facilitator briefing note. Total length 500 to 650 words.

**When to use it:** Monday morning when your HRBP has flagged that three new team leaders start in a fortnight and nobody has a plan.

**Pro tip:** If your 360 data is thin, paste in verbatim comments from exit interviews instead — the AI will extract themes and build the rationale around them, which makes the business case much easier to defend in sign-off.

> You are an HR Manager drafting an onboarding-to-management transition programme outline.
>
> Role being transitioned into: {new_role_title}
> Department: {department}
> Previous role held: {previous_role}
> Transition timeline: {transition_length} (e.g. 30 days, 90 days)
> Reported direct reports count: {direct_reports_count}
> Key tension to address (e.g. managing former peers): {key_tension}
> Manager's learning style preference: {learning_style} (e.g. self-directed, structured classroom, coaching)
>
> Write a personalised transition programme outline for this individual. Structure it in three phases: Week 1 orientation, Weeks 2 to 4 skill activation, and Month 2 onward embedding. Each phase should have two specific activities with a named output the manager produces (e.g. a team charter, a one-to-one schedule). Include a 75-word note on how to handle the specific tension listed. Total 400 to 500 words.

**When to use it:** When an IC has just been promoted and you need to hand something structured to their line manager before their first day in the new seat.

**Pro tip:** The "managing former peers" scenario is the one that trips up this prompt if you leave {key_tension} vague. Write it as a full sentence, e.g. "Two of her direct reports were her closest friends and openly celebrated her promotion — this may create boundary issues." The AI gives you a much more targeted activity if it knows the actual dynamic.

> You are writing a half-day training session outline for newly promoted frontline supervisors.
>
> Organisation type: {organisation_type} (e.g. NHS trust, logistics company, retail chain)
> Number of attendees: {attendee_count}
> Core topic: giving feedback to hourly-paid or shift workers
> Common scenario supervisors avoid: {avoided_scenario} (e.g. lateness conversations, uniform non-compliance)
> Facilitator background: {facilitator_background} (e.g. internal HR BP, external trainer, line manager)
> Room setup available: {room_setup} (e.g. cabaret, theatre, virtual breakout rooms)
>
> Write a half-day session outline (09:00 to 13:00) with timed segments, a named activity for each segment, the facilitator instruction for each activity in one sentence, and the learning outcome each activity addresses. Use the SBI feedback model throughout. Total 350 to 450 words.

**When to use it:** When an ops director asks you to "run something quick" for their supervisors after a spike in informal grievances.

**Pro tip:** Specify a real avoided scenario in {avoided_scenario} — not a category like "difficult conversations." The AI builds the role-play and debrief questions around that exact scenario, which saves you 20 minutes of rewriting activities to make them feel real.

> You are an HR Manager writing a training programme outline for new managers who have inherited an underperforming team.
>
> Team size inherited: {team_size}
> Industry sector: {sector}
> Current team performance issue: {performance_issue} (one sentence)
> Manager's tenure in the organisation: {manager_tenure}
> Programme format: {format} (e.g. four 90-minute sessions over four weeks)
> HR support available during programme: {hr_support_level} (e.g. HR BP attends session 1 only, full HR partnership)
>
> Write a four-session training programme outline. For each session include: session title, 90-minute agenda broken into three blocks, one skill the manager practises in the session, and one action they commit to before the next session. Add a 60-word note for the HR BP on what to watch for across the four sessions. Total 450 to 550 words.

**When to use it:** When a manager has been parachuted into a team flagged on your engagement data and you need to support them before they make it worse.

**Pro tip:** Fill {hr_support_level} honestly. If HR won't be in the room, the AI removes activities that require an HR co-facilitator and replaces them with self-directed tools the manager can use alone. This matters more than it sounds.

> You are drafting a training programme brief for an external provider to quote against.
>
> Programme title: {programme_title}
> Target audience: new managers, {headcount} people, average tenure {average_tenure}
> Business problem the programme must solve: {business_problem} (two sentences max)
> Budget ceiling: {budget_ceiling}
> Preferred delivery window: {delivery_window} (e.g. Q3 2026, within 8 weeks)
> Non-negotiable inclusion: {non_negotiable_topic} (e.g. employment law basics, psychosocial risk awareness)
>
> Write a provider brief that includes: a one-paragraph context summary, five numbered learning outcomes, three constraints the provider must design within (budget, timeline, non-negotiable topic), and a 75-word evaluation criteria section the provider must address in their response. Tone: professional and direct. Total 350 to 450 words.

**When to use it:** When procurement is pushing you to go to market but you haven't had time to write the spec.

**Pro tip:** Put a real number in {budget_ceiling} even if it's a rough ceiling. Vague budget fields produce vague scope — the AI won't write a meaningful constraints section if it doesn't know what it's constraining against.

## Compliance and Mandatory Training Outlines

> You are an HR Manager writing a mandatory training programme outline on workplace harassment prevention.
>
> Jurisdiction: {jurisdiction} (e.g. England and Wales, New South Wales, Ontario)
> Employee level: {employee_level} (e.g. all staff, managers only, board level)
> Trigger for programme: {trigger} (e.g. new legislation, recent complaint, routine refresh)
> Duration allocated: {duration} (e.g. 2 hours, half day)
> Delivery channel: {delivery_channel} (e.g. e-learning module, live facilitated, hybrid)
> Tone required: {tone} (e.g. legally precise, accessible plain English, trauma-informed)
>
> Write a training programme outline that includes: a legal context paragraph naming the specific jurisdiction, four learning objectives written as behavioural outcomes, a content plan broken into timed sections, three real scenario descriptions (no names) for use in breakout or reflection activities, and a 60-word note on documentation requirements post-training. Total 500 to 600 words.

**When to use it:** When legal or the CEO asks HR to "sort out harassment training" the week after a policy update lands.

**Pro tip:** Name the specific legislation in {jurisdiction} if you know it — e.g. "England and Wales — Worker Protection (Amendment of Equality Act 2010) Act 2023." The AI writes a sharper legal context paragraph and flags what the training must cover to meet the duty to prevent, rather than giving you a generic equal opportunities outline.

> You are writing a data protection and GDPR refresher training outline for non-technical staff.
>
> Organisation size: {organisation_size}
> Staff roles covered: {staff_roles} (e.g. customer service advisors, warehouse operatives, admin staff)
> Last training date: {last_training_date}
> Recent incident or near-miss: {recent_incident} (one sentence, anonymised)
> Time available: {time_available} (e.g. 45 minutes, lunchtime session)
> Delivery method: {delivery_method} (e.g. team meeting slot, standalone e-learn, toolbox talk)
>
> Write a refresher training outline for this session. Include: a one-paragraph hook that references the recent incident without naming individuals, three bite-sized content blocks each with a title and two key points, one group discussion question per block, and a 50-word closing action prompt staff take away. Plain English throughout. Total 300 to 400 words.

**When to use it:** After your DPO flags a near-miss and asks HR to push a refresher before the quarter ends.

**Pro tip:** Don't leave {recent_incident} blank or write "N/A." Even a fictional but realistic incident (e.g. "a staff member emailed a customer list to their personal account by mistake") produces a hook that makes non-technical staff actually pay attention. A blank field produces a generic GDPR lecture.

> You are an HR Manager writing a health and safety induction training outline for a new site.
>
> Site type: {site_type} (e.g. food production facility, distribution warehouse, office building)
> Regulatory body: {regulatory_body} (e.g. HSE, SafeWork Australia, WorkSafe BC)
> Induction length allocated: {induction_length} (e.g. 3 hours on day one)
> High-risk areas on site: {high_risk_areas} (bullet list of three)
> Person delivering induction: {induction_lead} (e.g. site manager, HR, external H&S consultant)
> Languages spoken by new starters: {languages} (e.g. English only, English and Polish, multilingual)
>
> Write a site induction training outline broken into timed blocks. Each block must include: title, duration, delivery method, one compliance checkpoint the induction lead must confirm before moving on, and the document or form completed at that stage. Include a 75-word note on language and accessibility adaptations. Total 400 to 500 words.

**When to use it:** Three days before a new site opens and the ops manager is asking what the induction plan looks like.

**Pro tip:** If {languages} is multilingual, the AI will flag which sections need dual-language materials and which compliance checkpoints need a verified interpreter present. This saves you a governance headache if there's ever a post-incident review.

> You are drafting a fire safety and emergency procedures training outline for a mixed-tenure workforce.
>
> Building type: {building_type} (e.g. multi-floor office, retail store, hospital ward)
> New staff percentage in cohort: {new_staff_percentage} (e.g. 60% new starters, 40% refreshers)
> Last full evacuation drill: {last_drill_date}
> Any accessibility requirements in the cohort: {accessibility_needs} (e.g. one wheelchair user, two staff with hearing impairments)
> Duration: {duration} (e.g. 1 hour including drill)
> Warden names to reference: {warden_names} (first names only)
>
> Write a combined new-starter and refresher fire safety training outline. Structure it so new starters and experienced staff separate for the first 20 minutes, then rejoin for the drill. Include a timed agenda, one differentiated activity per group during the split phase, and a 50-word briefing note for the named wardens. Total 300 to 380 words.

**When to use it:** When you're running a joint induction cohort and don't want experienced staff sitting through basics they already know.

**Pro tip:** Add the warden names in {warden_names} even if it feels minor. The AI writes the briefing note to them by name, which means they actually read it rather than treating it as a generic handout.

> You are writing a manual handling training outline for a warehouse team with a recent injury record.
>
> Injury type recorded: {injury_type} (e.g. three lower back strains in Q1 2026)
> Team size: {team_size}
> Shift pattern: {shift_pattern} (e.g. four on four off, rotating days and nights)
> Trainer qualification: {trainer_qualification} (e.g. internal first aider, external physio, qualified H&S trainer)
> Max session length per shift: {max_session_length} (e.g. 45 minutes at shift handover)
> Physical environment constraint: {environment_constraint} (e.g. no dedicated training room, must happen on the floor)
>
> Write a practical on-the-floor manual handling training outline designed for a 45-minute handover slot. Include: an opening two-minute briefing script the trainer reads aloud, four practical stations with the equipment needed and the one error each station corrects, and a 60-word closing note linking the training to the specific injury record. Total 350 to 450 words.

**When to use it:** After a RIDDOR reportable injury and before the HSE follow-up visit, when you need evidence that corrective training happened fast.

**Pro tip:** The opening briefing script is the most valuable output in this prompt. Read it yourself before handing it to the trainer — if it references the injuries in a way that feels accusatory rather than protective, adjust the {injury_type} wording to frame it as "we've seen three incidents across the site" not "your team has had three injuries."

## Soft Skills and Leadership Development Outlines

> You are an L&D Manager writing a communication skills training programme outline for middle managers.
>
> Organisation: {organisation_name}
> Specific communication failure driving this: {communication_failure} (one sentence, e.g. "managers are not cascading board decisions clearly and teams feel uninformed")
> Cohort size: {cohort_size}
> Programme format: {format} (e.g. three half-day workshops across six weeks)
> Current manager confidence level: {confidence_level} (e.g. low — most avoid difficult conversations, mixed, generally confident but inconsistent)
> Primary stakeholder who commissioned this: {stakeholder} (e.g. COO, Chief People Officer, General Manager)
>
> Write a three-workshop programme outline. For each workshop include: a title that names the specific skill (not "communication workshop 1"), a 60-word rationale, four timed session blocks with activity descriptions, and one manager commitment made at the close. End with a 100-word ROI summary paragraph the HR Manager can use when presenting back to the commissioning stakeholder. Total 550 to 650 words.

**When to use it:** When engagement survey results show "my manager doesn't keep me informed" is in the bottom quartile and someone above you is asking what L&D is doing about it.
