## Senior Role Postings

> You are writing a job description for a senior-level position at a growing company.
> 
> Role: {job_title}
> Department: {department}
> Reports to: {manager_title}
> Team size they'll manage: {number_of_reports}
> Years of experience required: {experience_range}
> Key challenge they'll solve: {primary_business_problem}
> Company stage: {startup / scale-up / enterprise}
> Location: {location_details}
> Salary range: {compensation_range}
> 
> Write a 400-500 word job description that opens with the business impact this role will have. Structure it with: role summary (2 sentences), key responsibilities (5-6 bullets), must-have qualifications (4-5 bullets), and what makes this opportunity unique (3-4 bullets). Use confident language that attracts ambitious candidates.

**When to use it:** When you're hiring for director-level or above roles where candidates need to understand strategic impact, not just tasks.

**Pro tip:** Always lead with the business problem they'll solve. Senior candidates care more about impact than perks.

> Required experience: {specific_background}
> Industry: {industry_context}
> Company size: {employee_count}
> Growth stage: {funding_round_or_revenue_stage}
> 
> Write a 350-450 word job description that emphasizes ownership and autonomy. Open with the transformation they'll lead. Include 6 core responsibilities focused on outcomes, 5 technical requirements, and 4 leadership qualifications. End with growth opportunity and equity upside.

**When to use it:** For VP of Engineering, CTO, or Head of Product roles where you need to attract proven leaders, not just experienced ICs.

**Pro tip:** Mention specific technologies in your stack. Vague "experience with modern frameworks" attracts weak candidates.
