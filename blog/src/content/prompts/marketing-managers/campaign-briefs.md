## Product Launch Campaign Briefs

> You are a marketing manager preparing a product launch campaign brief for leadership approval.
> 
> Product: {product_name}
> Launch date: {launch_date}
> Target audience: {primary_audience_demographics}
> Budget range: {budget_amount}
> Key product benefits: {three_main_benefits}
> Competitive landscape: {main_competitors}
> Success metrics: {kpis_and_targets}
> Channel mix: {channels_to_use}
> Risk factors: {potential_obstacles}
> 
> Write a 450-500 word campaign brief using the situation-challenge-solution structure. Open with market context and opportunity size. Detail the campaign strategy in the middle section with specific tactics for each channel. Close with timeline milestones and measurement plan.

**When to use it:** Three weeks before your launch when you need leadership sign-off on budget and strategy.

**Pro tip:** Include competitive response scenarios in your risk factors. Leadership always asks "what if competitor X does Y during our launch?"

> You are a marketing manager creating a soft launch campaign brief for product testing.
> 
> Product: {product_name}
> Test markets: {geographic_regions}
> Test duration: {timeline_weeks}
> Sample size target: {number_of_users}
> Learning objectives: {what_you_want_to_discover}
> Budget allocation: {spend_per_channel}
> Feedback collection method: {surveys_interviews_analytics}
> Scale-up criteria: {success_thresholds}
> Team stakeholders: {key_people_involved}
> 
> Write a 350-400 word soft launch brief structured around learn-test-iterate methodology. Lead with hypothesis to test. Outline measurement framework in middle section. End with decision criteria for full launch.

**When to use it:** When you're running a limited market test before committing to a full campaign rollout.

**Pro tip:** Set clear "go/no-go" thresholds upfront. Soft launches fail when teams can't agree whether results justify scaling up.

> 
> Holiday period: {specific_holiday_season}
> Product focus: {featured_products}
> Shopping behavior shift: {how_customers_change}
> Competitive intensity: {market_saturation_level}
> Promotion strategy: {discount_bundle_gift_approach}
> Key shopping dates: {black_friday_cyber_monday_etc}
> Inventory considerations: {stock_levels_supply_chain}
> Customer segments: {gift_givers_self_purchasers}

> 
> Write a 500-550 word holiday campaign brief using chronological structure. Start with pre-season awareness building. Detail peak shopping period tactics in middle section. End with post-holiday retention strategy.

**When to use it:** Eight weeks before major holiday seasons when you need cross-functional alignment on promotions and inventory.

**Pro tip:** Map your campaign timeline to competitor promotion calendars. Going first or last can be more effective than fighting in the middle.
