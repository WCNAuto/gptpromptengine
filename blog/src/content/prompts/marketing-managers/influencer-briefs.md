## Campaign Launch Briefs

> You are a Marketing Manager writing an influencer brief for a new product launch campaign.
> 
> Product: {product_name}
> Launch date: {launch_date}
> Target audience: {demographic_and_psychographics}
> Campaign budget: {total_budget_range}
> Influencer tier: {micro_influencers / mid_tier / macro / celebrity}
> Key message: {main_positioning_statement}
> Deliverables needed: {number_and_type_of_posts}
> Campaign hashtags: {branded_hashtags}
> 
> Write a 400-500 word influencer campaign brief that includes campaign objectives, content requirements, posting timeline, usage rights, and payment terms. Structure it with clear sections and bullet points for easy scanning. Include specific do's and don'ts for content creation.

**When to use it:** When you're 48 hours out from sending briefs to your shortlisted creators and need the foundational campaign document finished.

**Pro tip:** Always specify usage rights duration upfront - most creator pushback comes from unclear licensing terms buried in contracts later.

>

> Skin type focus: {target_skin_concerns}
> Application technique: {specific_usage_instructions}
> Before/after timeline: {results_timeframe}
> Lighting requirements: {natural / ring_light / studio}
> Competitor restrictions: {brands_to_avoid_mentioning}
> Claims compliance: {approved_benefit_statements}
> Tutorial length: {video_duration_range}
> 
> Write a 400-450 word guideline document covering proper product application, compliant benefit claims, visual standards, and authentic integration tips. Include a section on what not to say and approved messaging framework. Structure with numbered steps and clear compliance boundaries.

**When to use it:** Before sending skincare PR packages to creators who haven't worked with your brand before and need detailed usage guidance.

**Pro tip:** Include close-up application shots in your requirements - beauty audiences want to see texture, absorption, and technique details.
