## Problem-Solution Hooks

> You are writing a YouTube Shorts hook that presents a common problem and teases the solution.
> 
> Topic: {your_video_topic}
> Target audience: {specific_audience_like_students_parents_entrepreneurs}
> Problem they face: {specific_pain_point}
> Your solution: {what_you_teach_in_the_video}
> Urgency factor: {why_this_matters_now}
> Your credibility: {relevant_experience_or_result}
> Tone: {conversational / authoritative / empathetic}
> 
> Write a 15-20 word hook that opens with the problem, creates urgency, and teases your solution without giving it away. Start with "If you're {audience}" or "Stop {doing_wrong_thing}" or "{Number}% of {audience} don't know this". End with a question or cliffhanger that makes them want to keep watching.

**When to use it:** When you're teaching something that solves a specific frustration your audience deals with weekly.

**Pro tip:** Test hooks that start with "Stop" vs "If you're" - stop-pattern interrupts often perform 20-30% better for how-to content.

> Video length: {30_seconds / 60_seconds}

> 
> Write a 12-18 word hook that calls out the mistake immediately, hints at the cost of continuing, and promises the right way. Use "You're doing {thing} wrong" or "Everyone thinks {thing} but actually" or "This mistake costs you {consequence}". Make it feel like urgent insider knowledge.

**When to use it:** When you see the same mistake repeatedly in your niche and have a clear fix to share.

**Pro tip:** Include a specific number or timeframe in your hook - "This costs you 3 hours" performs better than "This wastes time".

> Proof: {data_screenshot_video_evidence}
> Your background: {why_you_were_in_position_to_discover_this}

**When to use it:** When you have a genuine unexpected result from testing, experimenting, or trying something new.

**Pro tip:** Film your actual reaction to the discovery if possible - authentic surprise emotions drive more engagement than scripted reveals.

## Urgency and FOMO Hooks

> You are writing a YouTube Shorts hook about a time-sensitive opportunity or change.
> 
> Opportunity: {what_is_available_now}
> Deadline: {when_it_ends_or_changes}
> Why it's ending: {reason_for_time_limit}
> Who benefits: {ideal_person_for_this}
> Barrier to entry: {what_makes_it_exclusive}
> Your connection: {how_you_know_about_this}
> Action required: {what_viewers_need_to_do}
> 
> Create a 15-22 word hook that creates urgency without being pushy. Use "This ends {when} and most people have no idea" or "If you're {audience}, you have {timeframe} left to {action}" or "I wish someone told me about this before it was too late". Focus on helping them avoid regret, not pressuring them to buy.

**When to use it:** When there's a genuine deadline or limited-time situation that benefits your audience.

**Pro tip:** Explain why the deadline exists - artificial urgency kills trust, but logical urgency drives action.
