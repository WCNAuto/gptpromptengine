## Product Review Descriptions

> You are writing a YouTube description for a product review video.

> Product: {product_name}
> Video length: {duration_in_minutes}
> Main pros: {three_key_benefits}
> Main cons: {two_main_drawbacks}
> Price point: {price_range}
> Target keyword: {primary_seo_keyword}
> Your channel tone: {professional / casual / tech-focused / lifestyle}
> Affiliate status: {yes_with_link / no_affiliate / sponsored}

> Write a 200-250 word YouTube description optimized for {primary_seo_keyword}. Start with a hook that includes the keyword in the first 15 words. Include timestamps for pros (around 2:30) and cons (around 5:45). Add 8-10 relevant hashtags. End with a clear subscribe call-to-action tied to reviewing similar products.

**When to use it:** Right after filming your product review, when you need the description live within your upload workflow.

**Pro tip:** Place your affiliate disclosure in the first two sentences if required by law. YouTube's algorithm doesn't penalize early disclosures, but late ones can trigger manual reviews.

> You are writing a YouTube description comparing two competing products.

> Product A: {first_product_name}
> Product B: {second_product_name}
> Category: {product_category}
> Winner: {which_product_wins}
> Key differentiator: {main_reason_for_winner}
> Target keyword: {comparison_keyword_phrase}
> Video structure: {intro_comparison_verdict / side_by_side / feature_breakdown}
> Price difference: {price_gap_and_reasoning}

> Write a 180-220 word description for YouTube optimized around {comparison_keyword_phrase}. Open with the verdict in the first sentence to hook searchers. Include timestamps for each product's showcase. Use 6-8 hashtags mixing product names and category terms. Close with a question asking viewers which product they'd choose.

**When to use it:** For head-to-head comparison videos where viewers are actively searching for buying guidance between specific products.

**Pro tip:** Lead with your conclusion, not a teaser. Comparison searchers want the answer fast, and YouTube rewards descriptions that match search intent immediately.

## Tutorial and How-To Descriptions

> You are writing a YouTube description for a step-by-step tutorial video.

> Tutorial topic: {what_you_are_teaching}
> Difficulty level: {beginner / intermediate / advanced}
> Time to complete: {how_long_the_task_takes}
> Key steps: {step_1} {step_2} {step_3} {step_4}
> Common mistakes: {main_error_people_make}
> Target keyword: {how_to_keyword_phrase}
> Tools needed: {required_equipment_or_software}

> Write a 220-260 word YouTube description optimized for {how_to_keyword_phrase}. Start with "Learn to [accomplish goal] in [time_to_complete]." List each major step with timestamps. Include a tools/requirements section. Use 8-10 hashtags mixing tutorial terms and topic-specific keywords. End with encouragement and a request for viewers to share their results.

**When to use it:** Right after recording any instructional content where viewers need to follow along step-by-step.

**Pro tip:** Put the completion time in the first sentence. Tutorial searchers want to know the time commitment before they click, and YouTube prioritizes descriptions that answer the viewer's unspoken questions.

> You are writing a YouTube description for a troubleshooting or problem-solving video.

> Problem being solved: {specific_issue_addressed}
> Common causes: {why_this_problem_happens}
> Your solution: {the_fix_you_demonstrate}
> Time to fix: {how_long_the_solution_takes}
> Difficulty rating: {easy / moderate / requires_experience}
> Target keyword: {problem_solving_keyword_phrase}
> Tools required: {what_viewers_need_to_follow_along}

> Write a 190-230 word YouTube description targeting {problem_solving_keyword_phrase}. Open with "If you're struggling with [specific_issue_addressed], this fixes it." Explain the cause briefly, then jump to your solution with a timestamp. Include a difficulty warning if relevant. Use 7-9 hashtags including #fix and problem-specific terms. Close by asking viewers to confirm if this solved their issue.

**When to use it:** For videos solving specific pain points that your audience frequently asks about in comments or DMs.

**Pro tip:** Include both the problem and solution keywords in your description. People search for problems ("why is my X doing Y") and solutions ("how to fix X") differently.

## Entertainment and Lifestyle Descriptions

> You are writing a YouTube description for a day-in-the-life or routine video.

> Routine type: {morning / work / evening / weekend / special_occasion}
> Unique angle: {what_makes_your_routine_interesting}
> Key moments: {highlight_1} {highlight_2} {highlight_3}
> Personal insight: {what_viewers_learn_about_you}
> Time period covered: {duration_of_routine_shown}
> Target keyword: {routine_lifestyle_keyword}
> Call to action: {what_you_want_viewers_to_do}

> Write a 180-220 word YouTube description targeting {routine_lifestyle_keyword}. Open with "Here's what a real [routine_type] looks like for [your_situation]." Highlight the most interesting or relatable moments with timestamps. Include one vulnerable or surprising detail. Use 7-9 hashtags mixing lifestyle and routine-specific terms. End by asking viewers to share one thing from their own routine.

**When to use it:** For personal content where you're building connection and relatability with your audience through everyday moments.

**Pro tip:** Include one unexpected element in your routine description. "Routine" content can feel predictable, so hint at something surprising to improve click-through rates.
