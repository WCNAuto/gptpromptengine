## Initial Brand Outreach

> You are a YouTuber pitching a brand partnership for the first time.
> 
> Channel name: {channel_name}
> Your niche: {niche}
> Subscriber count: {subscriber_count}
> Average views per video: {average_views}
> Brand you're pitching: {brand_name}
> Why you chose them: {brand_connection_reason}
> Your audience demographic: {audience_age_and_gender}
> Previous brand work: {yes_with_examples / no_but_eager}
> 
> Write a 200 to 250 word cold outreach email. Open with a specific reason you love their brand, not generic praise. Include your key metrics in one tight sentence. Close with a specific collaboration idea, not "let's work together". Use confident but not pushy tone.

**When to use it:** When you've found a perfect brand match and need to make first contact without sounding like every other YouTuber in their inbox.

**Pro tip:** Mention a specific product you actually own and use. Brands can spot fake enthusiasm from space.

> You are a YouTuber following up on an initial sponsorship inquiry that got no response.
> 
> Channel name: {channel_name}
> Brand name: {brand_name}
> Days since first email: {days_since_contact}
> New achievement since then: {recent_milestone}
> Specific video that would fit: {video_idea}
> Your main value proposition: {unique_selling_point}
> 
> Write a 150 to 180 word follow-up email. Reference your previous email briefly without being needy. Lead with your new achievement. Propose one specific video concept that showcases their product naturally. End with a soft deadline.

**When to use it:** Two weeks after your first email when you need to re-surface without being annoying.

**Pro tip:** Always include a new piece of information or achievement. Never send "just checking in" follow-ups.

**When to use it:** When you're under 50K subscribers but have killer engagement and want to punch above your weight class.

**Pro tip:** Lead with engagement rate, not subscriber count. A 10% engagement rate beats 100K dead followers every time.

## Partnership Proposal Development

> You are a YouTuber creating a detailed partnership proposal for a brand that showed initial interest.
> 
> Channel name: {channel_name}
> Brand name: {brand_name}
> Proposed video type: {video_format}
> Deliverables package: {main_video_plus_extras}
> Timeline: {production_and_delivery_schedule}
> Your rate: {total_package_price}
> Usage rights offered: {platform_and_duration}
> Performance guarantees: {minimum_views_or_engagement}
> Previous brand work results: {case_study_metrics}
> 
> Write a 400 to 500 word formal partnership proposal. Structure with clear sections: Overview, Deliverables, Timeline, Investment, Performance History. Make it scannable with bullet points. Include specific metrics from past sponsorships. End with clear next steps.

**When to use it:** When a brand requests a formal proposal after initial interest and you need to close the deal.

**Pro tip:** Include view guarantees only if you're confident. Under-promise and over-deliver beats the reverse.

> You are a YouTuber proposing a long-term brand partnership instead of one-off sponsorship.
> 
> Channel name: {channel_name}
> Brand name: {brand_name}
> Proposed duration: {partnership_length}
> Content frequency: {videos_per_month}
> Exclusivity terms: {competitor_exclusions}
> Quarterly goals: {growth_and_engagement_targets}
> Seasonal content ideas: {holiday_or_event_tie_ins}
> Discount for commitment: {reduced_rate_percentage}
> Performance benchmarks: {success_metrics}
> 
> Write a 350 to 400 word proposal for ongoing partnership. Emphasize brand building over quick sales. Include content calendar sketch. Offer performance improvements over time as audience grows. Structure pricing to reward their commitment with better rates.

**When to use it:** When you want stable income and a brand seems perfect for ongoing collaboration.

**Pro tip:** Propose quarterly reviews instead of annual contracts. Brands prefer shorter commitments with renewal options.

**Pro tip:** Research their existing product line thoroughly. Your idea should fill a gap, not compete with existing products.

> You are a YouTuber counter-proposing after a brand offered terms that don't work for you.
> 
> Original offer details: {what_they_proposed}
> Your counter on rate: {your_preferred_rate}
> Timeline concerns: {why_their_deadline_does_not_work}
> Creative control requests: {approval_process_you_want}
> Additional deliverables you will include: {extras_to_sweeten_deal}
> Compromise areas: {where_you_can_be_flexible}
> Non-negotiables: {what_you_cannot_change}
> 
> Write a 250 to 300 word counter-proposal email that feels collaborative, not combative. Acknowledge what works in their offer. Clearly explain why you're asking for changes. Offer additional value where possible. Maintain enthusiasm for working together.

**When to use it:** When a brand's initial offer is close but needs adjustment on rate, timeline, or creative terms.

**Pro tip:** Never counter without adding something. If you want more money, include extra deliverables or extended usage rights.

## Rate Cards and Pricing

> You are a YouTuber creating a rate card for potential sponsors.
> 
> Channel name: {channel_name}
> Subscriber count: {current_subscribers}
> Average video views: {typical_view_count}
> Engagement rate: {average_engagement_percentage}
> Content categories: {your_main_content_types}
> Dedicated video rate: {full_sponsored_video_price}
> Integration rate: {product_mention_price}
> Package deal discount: {multi_video_discount_percentage}
> Additional services: {extra_deliverables_and_pricing}
> 
> Create a 200 to 250 word professional rate card. Present pricing confidently without justification. Include package deals that incentivize multiple bookings. List additional services like Instagram posts or YouTube Shorts. Use clean formatting that's easy to scan.

**When to use it:** When brands ask for your rates or you want to include pricing in initial outreach.

**Pro tip:** Always include package pricing. Brands think in campaigns, not individual videos.

> You are a YouTube creator explaining why your rates are higher than typical industry calculators suggest.
> 
> Your rate per 1000 views: {your_cpm_equivalent}
> Industry average: {standard_rate_comparison}
> Your engagement rate: {your_superior_engagement}
> Audience quality factors: {demographics_income_loyalty}
> Production value: {video_quality_factors}
> Additional deliverables included: {extras_in_base_rate}
> Results from past sponsors: {performance_examples}
> 
> Write a 250 to 300 word explanation of your premium pricing. Focus on results, not costs. Compare audience quality, not just size. Include specific examples of sponsor success. Avoid defensive tone—educate on value instead.

**When to use it:** When a brand questions your rates or tries to negotiate based on industry averages.

**Pro tip:** Never lower rates just to win business. Discount by adding deliverables, not reducing price.

> You are a small YouTuber setting rates for your first paid sponsorships.
> 
> Channel size: {subscriber_count_under_10k}
> Average views: {typical_views_per_video}
> Engagement metrics: {comments_likes_shares_per_video}
> Content production time: {hours_spent_per_video}
> Your minimum acceptable rate: {lowest_you_will_go}
> What you'll include: {deliverables_package}
> Rate increase schedule: {when_and_how_rates_will_rise}
> 
> Create a 180 to 220 word rate card that prices fairly for your size while establishing value. Emphasize engagement over reach. Include timeline for rate increases as you grow. Bundle complementary deliverables. Price above minimum wage for your time invested.

**When to use it:** When you're starting out and need to price competitively but not desperately.

**Pro tip:** Start with product-only deals to build a portfolio, then introduce cash rates with proof of results.

**When to use it:** When dealing with brands that have substantial budgets and want comprehensive partnerships.

**Pro tip:** Premium packages should cost 3-5x basic rates but deliver 10x the value perception.

**Pro tip:** Grandfather existing sponsors for 3-6 months, then gradually transition to new rates.
