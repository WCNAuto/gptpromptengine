## Interview Episode Show Notes

> You are a podcast content manager writing show notes for an interview episode.

> Episode title: {episode_title}
> Guest name: {guest_name}
> Guest expertise: {guest_background}
> Episode length: {duration_minutes} minutes
> Main topics covered: {three_to_four_key_topics}
> Best quotes from guest: {two_memorable_quotes}
> Links mentioned: {relevant_links_or_resources}
> Target audience: {listener_demographic}
> Tone: {conversational / professional / casual}

> Write 400 to 500 word show notes that open with a compelling hook about why this guest matters to the audience. Include a bulleted topic breakdown, highlight the best quotes in pull-quote format, and close with clear next steps for listeners. Add timestamps for major topic shifts.

**When to use it:** Right after recording an interview when you need show notes published within 24 hours for maximum algorithm impact.

**Pro tip:** Always lead with the guest's biggest credibility marker in the opening line—it hooks browsers scanning multiple podcast options.

**Pro tip:** Use phrases like "explores the debate around" instead of "argues for"—it keeps you neutral while promising valuable content.

> Step-by-step process covered: {main_steps_taught}
> Common mistakes addressed: {pitfalls_warned_against}
> Tools or resources needed: {requirements}
> Time to implement: {realistic_timeframe}
> Difficulty level: {beginner / intermediate / advanced}
> Success metric: {how_to_measure_progress}

> Write 350 to 450 word show notes that position this as a mini-course. Open with the skill's value and your credibility. Outline the learning path clearly. Include implementation guidance and realistic expectations. Close with a specific first step listeners can take today.

**When to use it:** After recording a tutorial episode when you need to convince browsers this delivers real skill-building value, not just theory.

**Pro tip:** Include your failure count when learning this skill—"I made these mistakes so you don't have to" builds trust and relatability.

> Debate topic: {central_question_or_issue}
> Participants: {names_and_brief_credentials}
> Number of guests: {participant_count}
> Main positions represented: {different_viewpoints}
> Most heated exchange: {conflict_moment}
> Surprising agreement: {unexpected_consensus}
> Unresolved questions: {what_remains_debated}
> Episode length: {duration_minutes}

> Write 400 to 500 word show notes that capture the debate energy. Open with the stakes of the topic and credibility of participants. Map out the main positions clearly. Highlight the most dramatic exchange without spoiling outcomes. Close with questions listeners should consider.

**When to use it:** After recording a multi-guest debate when you need to convey the intellectual fireworks while helping listeners follow the different positions.

**Pro tip:** Name the participants' positions in shorthand early—"the skeptic," "the optimist," "the pragmatist"—so listeners can track who's talking.
