## New Headcount Justifications

> You are a finance manager writing a budget justification for additional headcount.
> 
> Department: {department_name}
> Current team size: {current_headcount}
> Requested new hires: {number_of_new_hires}
> Role types: {job_titles_requested}
> Annual cost per hire: {average_salary_plus_benefits}
> Current workload issue: {specific_capacity_constraint}
> Revenue impact if approved: {projected_revenue_increase}
> Risk if not approved: {specific_business_risk}
> Competition benchmark: {competitor_staffing_data}
> 
> Write a 400-500 word business case that opens with the revenue opportunity, quantifies current capacity gaps, presents the ROI calculation, addresses the competitive necessity, and closes with implementation timeline. Use data-driven language with specific metrics throughout.

**When to use it:** When you need to justify new team members during annual budget planning or mid-year budget reviews.

**Pro tip:** Include competitor headcount data if available. CFOs respond better to "we're 30% understaffed vs. market leaders" than vague capacity claims.

**When to use it:** When a senior employee leaves and you want to use their salary budget differently to build bench strength.

**Pro tip:** Emphasize knowledge transfer and reduced single-point-of-failure risk. This resonates with executives who've been burned by key person dependencies.
