## 24-Hour Flash Sale Announcements

> You are writing a flash sale announcement email for an ecommerce store.
> 
> Sale details: {sale_name}
> Products on sale: {product_categories}
> Discount: {percentage_or_dollar_amount}
> Duration: {exact_time_limit}
> Store name: {brand_name}
> Target audience: {customer_segment}
> Brand voice: {casual / premium / playful / professional}
> Inventory concern: {limited_stock / while_supplies_last / unlimited}
> 
> Write a 150-200 word email that creates urgency without being pushy. Start with a subject line. Use a countdown-style opener that mentions the exact time limit. Include one social proof element. End with a single, clear call-to-action button text and a PS line about the time limit.

**When to use it:** Monday morning when you need to blast a same-day flash sale announcement before lunch.

**Pro tip:** If your sale runs overnight, mention "while you sleep" in the subject line to catch late-night mobile browsers.

> Write a 180-250 word VIP early access email. Open by acknowledging their status. Clearly explain the advantage they're getting over regular customers. Include specific numbers comparing VIP vs public offers. Use "before everyone else" language. End with urgency about when public access begins.

**When to use it:** Thursday afternoon to give VIPs Friday early access before a weekend public sale.

**Pro tip:** Always state the exact public launch time in the email so VIPs feel the exclusivity window closing.

> 
> Sale trigger: {inventory_clearance / competitor_response / impulse_decision}
> Start time: {exact_time_and_timezone}

> Audience mood: {excitement / FOMO / treasure_hunt}

> Write a 120-160 word surprise flash sale email optimized for mobile reading. Use a mysterious subject line that doesn't give away the discount. Open with "breaking news" energy. Make the midnight start time feel like an event. Include one sentence about why you're doing this surprise sale. Keep paragraphs to one sentence each.

**When to use it:** Late afternoon when you decide to run an unplanned evening sale to boost weekly numbers.

**Pro tip:** Send this email at 6-7 PM so people see it during dinner when they're already on their phones.

> You are writing a flash sale extension email because the first sale was too popular.
> 
> Original sale name: {initial_sale_title}
> Original end time: {when_it_was_supposed_to_end}
> Extension length: {additional_hours_or_days}
> Reason for extension: {overwhelming_demand / technical_issues / customer_requests}
> Current stock status: {running_low / restocked / mixed}
> New end time: {final_deadline}
> Customer sentiment: {grateful / surprised / skeptical}
> Next sale timing: {mention_if_this_is_last_chance}
> 
> Write a 140-190 word extension announcement that doesn't feel like a bait-and-switch. Acknowledge the original deadline passed. Give a genuine reason for the extension. Create new urgency around the extended deadline. Reference the popularity that caused the extension. Include one line about what happens after this final deadline.

**When to use it:** When your sale performs so well you want to extend it, but need to maintain urgency for the new deadline.

**Pro tip:** Send the extension email 2-3 hours after the original deadline to catch people who missed it and thought they were out of luck.

> You are creating a "last call" flash sale email for the final 3 hours.
> 
> Sale name: {current_sale_title}
> Time remaining: {hours_and_minutes_left}
> Top performing products: {bestsellers_during_sale}
> Inventory alerts: {items_running_out}
> Discount percentage: {current_offer}
> Cart abandonment: {acknowledge_if_relevant}
> Post-sale pricing: {regular_price_return}
> Checkout process: {express / standard / mobile-optimized}
> 
> Write a 100-140 word final hours email that feels genuinely final. Use "last call" language from bars or events. List 2-3 items that are almost sold out. Include checkout time estimates to help people calculate if they can make it. Avoid repeated CTA buttons. Make it feel like the store is about to close forever. End with exact time remaining in minutes if under 4 hours.

**When to use it:** Final 3-4 hours of any flash sale when you need to convert browsers into buyers.

**Pro tip:** Mention checkout processing time ("orders take 2-3 minutes") so people know they can still make the deadline.

## Category-Specific Flash Promotions

> You are announcing a flash sale on seasonal items that need to move quickly.
> 
> Season: {current_season_ending}
> Product category: {clothing / decor / outdoor / holiday}
> Inventory situation: {overstock / last_season / transition_timing}
> Discount depth: {percentage_off}
> Comparison context: {next_year_pricing / storage_costs / freshness}
> Weather factor: {if_relevant_to_urgency}
> New season arrival: {when_next_inventory_comes}
> Target timeline: {hours_or_days_to_clear}
> 
> Write a 160-220 word seasonal clearance flash sale email. Explain why these prices won't happen again until next year. Use seasonal language that matches the current weather. Reference the incoming new season inventory. Make the old season feel special rather than outdated. Include specific product examples that illustrate the value.

**When to use it:** End of any season when you need to clear inventory before new arrivals take up warehouse space.

**Pro tip:** Reference weather patterns ("last warm weekend" or "first frost coming") to tie urgency to nature, not just artificial deadlines.

**When to use it:** When targeting people who found your site but haven't purchased yet, especially after high-traffic events.

## Urgency and Scarcity Frameworks

> You are writing a flash sale email about genuinely limited inventory that won't be restocked.
> 
> Product category: {discontinued_items / final_batch / vendor_change}
> Remaining quantity: {specific_numbers_or_very_low}
> Restocking status: {never / not_for_months / uncertain}
> Historical demand: {how_popular_these_items_were}
> Reason for limitation: {vendor_discontinue / seasonal_end / ingredient_shortage}
> Customer attachment: {cult_following / frequent_repurchases / high_reviews}
> Alternative options: {similar_products / substitutes / none}
> Price consideration: {last_chance_at_this_price}
> 
> Write a 160-210 word genuine scarcity email that doesn't feel manipulative. Explain the real reason for limited stock. Give specific quantities if very low. Acknowledge customer disappointment about no restocking. Suggest alternatives if they exist. Make it clear this isn't a marketing tactic. Use "final opportunity" language.

**When to use it:** When you're actually clearing out products that won't return, not creating artificial scarcity.

**Pro tip:** Include the real business reason (vendor stopped making it, ingredient sourcing issues) to make the scarcity feel authentic.

> Write a 150-200 word deadline-driven flash sale email. Combine sale urgency with shipping deadline urgency. Include a clear calendar date for last orders. Explain the consequences of missing the deadline. Mention rush shipping costs to make the deadline feel more valuable. Use event-specific language that matches the occasion.

**When to use it:** Week before major shipping deadlines when people need items to arrive by a specific date.

**Pro tip:** Include a calendar date, not "5 days from now" since people read emails at different times.
