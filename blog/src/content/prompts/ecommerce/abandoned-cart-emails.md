## First-Touch Recovery Emails

> You are writing the first email in an abandoned cart sequence for an ecommerce store.
> 
> Store name: {store_name}
> Product category: {product_category}
> Items in cart: {item_names_and_prices}
> Cart value: {total_cart_value}
> Customer first name: {customer_first_name}
> Store tone: {casual / professional / playful / luxury}
> Send timing: {1_hour / 3_hours / 6_hours} after abandonment
> Discount offer: {percentage_or_dollar_amount / no_discount}
> 
> Write a 150 to 200 word email that feels helpful, not pushy. Subject line that creates urgency without being aggressive. Remind them what they left behind, include one benefit of the main product, and end with a clear CTA button text suggestion. If offering a discount, frame it as "we noticed you hesitated" not "buy now for less".

**When to use it:** Monday morning when you're setting up the week's email campaigns and need the first touchpoint written fast.

**Pro tip:** Test subject lines with and without the customer's name. Some audiences respond better to "Your {product} is waiting" than "{First_name}, your cart is waiting".

> You are writing a gentle cart reminder email for a high-consideration purchase.
> 
> Store name: {store_name}
> Product type: {furniture / electronics / jewelry / other_expensive_item}
> Specific item abandoned: {product_name}
> Item price: {product_price}
> Customer name: {customer_first_name}
> Product's main benefit: {key_selling_point}
> Shipping promise: {free_shipping_threshold / delivery_timeframe}
> Store personality: {helpful / expert / premium / friendly}
> 
> Write a 120 to 180 word email that acknowledges this is a big decision. No discount offers. Focus on removing purchase anxiety with social proof, return policy, or expert consultation offer. Subject line should be consultative, not salesy. Include one specific product detail that justifies the price point.

**When to use it:** When your abandoned cart contains items over $300 where customers need reassurance more than discounts.

**Pro tip:** For high-ticket items, mention your customer service response time. "Questions? We respond within 2 hours" builds confidence.

> Customer name: {customer_first_name}

> Size availability: {only_few_sizes_left / full_size_run / one_size}

Include one styling tip for the abandoned item. End with clear next steps and size exchange policy if they're unsure about fit.

**When to use it:** Thursday afternoons when you're reviewing weekend inventory for fashion drops and need to move specific pieces.

**Pro tip:** Include a small product image in the email. Fashion customers often abandon carts because they forgot what the item looked like.

> You are writing a cart abandonment email for a customer who left multiple items from different categories.
> 
> Store name: {store_name}
> Items in cart: {mix_of_product_categories_and_prices}
> Total cart value: {total_amount}
> Customer name: {customer_first_name}
> Biggest ticket item: {most_expensive_product}
> Shipping threshold: {free_shipping_minimum}
> Store tone: {professional / friendly / premium / casual}
> Additional items needed for free shipping: {dollar_amount_to_reach_threshold}
> 
> Write a 140 to 180 word email that organizes their cart into a coherent story. Group items logically (outfit, room, project, etc.) Don't just list everything. Highlight if they're close to free shipping. Subject line that references the collection they built, not individual items. Include one cross-sell suggestion that completes their apparent intent.

**When to use it:** When cart analytics show customers building larger orders but abandoning before checkout completion.

**Pro tip:** Present mixed carts as curated collections. "Your home office setup" sounds intentional, "5 items in your cart" sounds random.

## Discount and Incentive Emails

> You are writing the second email in an abandoned cart sequence that introduces a discount.
> 
> Store name: {store_name}
> Days since first email: {1_day / 2_days / 3_days}
> Discount offer: {percentage_off / dollar_amount_off / free_shipping}
> Discount code: {promo_code}
> Expiration: {24_hours / 48_hours / weekend_only}
> Abandoned items: {product_names_and_original_prices}
> Customer name: {customer_first_name}
> Reason for discount: {first_time_customer / returning_customer / cart_value_threshold}
> Store voice: {helpful / direct / playful / sophisticated}
> 
> Write a 120 to 160 word email that frames the discount as solving a problem, not desperate selling. Subject line with the discount percentage but not all caps. Explain why they qualify for this offer. Include the exact savings amount in dollars, not just the percentage. Clear expiration date and simple redemption instructions.

**When to use it:** Wednesday morning when your Monday cart recovery emails haven't converted and you need to add urgency.

**Pro tip:** Show both the percentage and dollar amount saved. "$25 off (15%)" is more compelling than just "15% off".

> You are writing a cart recovery email offering free shipping as the incentive.
> 
> Store name: {store_name}
> Current cart value: {cart_total}
> Normal shipping cost: {shipping_fee}
> Free shipping threshold: {minimum_order_amount}
> Gap to free shipping: {additional_amount_needed}
> Customer location: {domestic / international}
> Shipping timeframe: {delivery_days}
> Abandoned items: {product_list}
> Customer name: {customer_first_name}
> 
> Write a 100 to 140 word email that positions free shipping as convenience, not just savings. If they're close to the threshold, suggest specific items to add. If they're far from threshold, offer free shipping as a special exception with clear expiration. Subject line that emphasizes convenience over savings. Include delivery timeframe to create urgency around timing.

**When to use it:** When shipping costs are the top abandonment reason in your checkout analytics.

**Pro tip:** For international customers, emphasize tracking and duties included, not just the free shipping savings.

> Flash sale discount: {percentage_or_amount}
> Sale duration: {4_hours / 12_hours / 24_hours}

> Items in cart: {product_names}
> Sale coverage: {entire_cart / specific_categories / minimum_purchase}
> Customer name: {customer_first_name}
> 
> Write a 90 to 130 word email that creates urgency without panic. Subject line with specific end time, not vague "limited time". Explain exactly what's included in the sale. Show their specific savings amount on their abandoned cart. Include countdown language but keep it sophisticated. Clear redemption instructions that work with their existing cart.

**When to use it:** Friday afternoon when you're launching weekend flash sales and want to capture fence-sitter revenue.

**Pro tip:** Send flash sale emails to abandoned cart customers 1 hour before your main list. Reward them for previous engagement.

**When to use it:** When selling consumable products where customers benefit from having backups or sharing with others.

**Pro tip:** For skincare BOGO offers, suggest using one item morning and one evening, or keeping one at home and one for travel.

## Social Proof and Urgency Emails

> You are writing a cart abandonment email featuring customer reviews for the specific abandoned product.
> 
> Store name: {store_name}
> Abandoned product: {specific_product_name}
> Product rating: {star_rating_out_of_5}
> Number of reviews: {total_review_count}
> Standout review quote: {specific_customer_review_excerpt}
> Reviewer name: {review_customer_first_name}
> Common praise theme: {durability / comfort / quality / value / appearance}
> Customer name: {customer_first_name}
> Product category: {item_type}
> Price point: {product_cost}
> 
> Write a 140 to 180 word email that lets other customers sell the product. Lead with the review quote, not your product description. Subject line that mentions real customer experiences. Include the specific rating and review count for credibility. End with one detail about what makes this product earn these reviews. Make the social proof feel authentic, not cherry-picked.

**When to use it:** When you have strong reviews for specific products and want to let satisfied customers do the convincing.

**Pro tip:** Use review quotes that mention specific use cases, not just "great product". "Perfect for my small apartment" is more convincing than "love it".

> You are writing an inventory alert email for genuinely low-stock abandoned cart items.
> 
> Store name: {store_name}
> Product with low inventory: {specific_item_name}
> Actual stock level: {specific_number_remaining}
> Size/variant breakdown: {which_sizes_colors_low}
> Customer's selected variant: {their_specific_choice}
> Restocking timeline: {weeks_until_restock / not_restocking / unknown}
> Product popularity: {bestseller / seasonal / discontinued}
> Customer name: {customer_first_name}
> Alternative options: {similar_products_in_stock}
> 
> Write a 100 to 140 word email that's helpful, not manipulative. Only send if inventory is genuinely low. Subject line with specific stock numbers. If their size is gone, suggest alternatives. If restocking is uncertain, be honest. Include one reason why this item sells out (quality, limited edition, seasonal demand). Make it about helping them get what they want, not pressuring them to buy.

**When to use it:** When inventory management alerts show specific abandoned cart items dropping below reorder points.

**Pro tip:** Include a "notify when restocked" option for out-of-stock variants. Capture the lead even if you can't make the sale.
