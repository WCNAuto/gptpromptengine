## Negative Review Recovery

> You are a customer service manager responding to a negative product review on {platform: Amazon / Google / Trustpilot / Yelp}.
> 
> Customer name: {customer_name}
> Product: {product_name}
> Main complaint: {specific_issue}
> Order date: {order_date}
> Your company name: {company_name}
> Desired outcome: {refund / replacement / store_credit / investigation}
> Brand tone: {professional / friendly / apologetic / solution_focused}
> 
> Write a 120 to 180 word public response that acknowledges the specific issue, takes responsibility without admitting legal fault, offers a concrete solution, and invites them to contact you directly. Include your direct contact method. End with a commitment to prevent future occurrences.

**When to use it:** When you spot a 1-3 star review that mentions specific product defects, shipping delays, or service failures.

**Pro tip:** Never copy-paste legal language about "alleged" issues. Customers spot corporate-speak instantly and it makes negative reviews go viral.

> You are responding to a 1-star review about damaged items during shipping.
> 
> Customer: {customer_name}
> Platform: {review_platform}
> Damage described: {broken_items_description}
> Order number: {order_number}
> Shipping carrier: {ups / fedex / usps / dhl}
> Your store name: {store_name}
> Response urgency: {same_day / within_24_hours}
> 
> Write a 100 to 150 word response that separates shipping damage from product quality, explains your packaging improvements, offers immediate replacement plus shipping upgrade, and provides your direct email for faster resolution. Use empathetic but confident tone.

**When to use it:** When shipping damage creates unfair negative reviews that could hurt your product ratings long-term.

**Pro tip:** Mention specific packaging upgrades you've made since their order date. Shows other customers you're actively improving.

**Pro tip:** Give exact response time commitments ("within 4 hours during business days") rather than vague promises about "faster service."

## Positive Review Amplification

> You are thanking a customer who left a detailed 5-star review highlighting specific product benefits.
> 
> Customer name: {customer_first_name}
> Product they reviewed: {product_name}
> Specific benefits they mentioned: {their_favorite_features}
> Platform: {review_platform}
> How long they've used it: {usage_timeframe}
> Your brand personality: {warm / professional / enthusiastic / minimalist}
> Related products: {complementary_items}
> 
> Write a 60 to 100 word response that thanks them specifically for the detailed feedback, highlights one feature they loved that other customers ask about, and subtly mentions a complementary product they might enjoy. Sound genuinely grateful, not sales-y.

**When to use it:** When power customers leave reviews that address common objections or highlight key selling points for hesitant buyers.

**Pro tip:** Quote their exact phrase about the benefit back to them. Shows you read their review personally and helps reinforce that benefit for future buyers.

**When to use it:** When customers specifically call out your service quality in ways that could influence other buyers' purchase confidence.

**Pro tip:** If they mention an employee by name, tag that person in your internal slack. Good service reviews deserve immediate team recognition.

## Moderate Review Management

> You are responding to a 3-star review where the customer likes the product but has one specific complaint.
> 
> Customer: {customer_name}
> Product: {product_name}
> What they like: {positive_aspects_mentioned}
> Specific complaint: {one_main_issue}
> Platform: {review_platform}
> Complaint type: {design_flaw / missing_feature / size_issue / usability_problem}
> Fixable issue: {yes / no}
> 
> Write a 100 to 140 word response thanking them for the balanced feedback, acknowledging their complaint as valid, explaining any available solutions or workarounds, and mentioning if you're considering product improvements. Aim to move them from 3 stars to 4 stars without asking directly.

**When to use it:** When lukewarm reviews contain fixable issues that could turn satisfied customers into advocates with the right follow-up.

**Pro tip:** Follow up privately via email with these customers 30 days later. They're most likely to upgrade their review if their issue gets resolved.

> Product: {product_name}

> Actual capability: {what_product_actually_does}

> Write a 120 to 170 word response that gently clarifies the product's intended use, acknowledges where your marketing could be clearer, offers helpful tips for getting better results within the product's actual capabilities, and provides return option if it's truly not what they need.

**When to use it:** When customers leave disappointed reviews based on misunderstanding what your product was designed to do.

**Pro tip:** Use these reviews to identify description gaps in your product pages. If one customer got confused, dozens more probably did too.

## Multi-Platform Reputation Control

> You are crafting a response to the same negative review that appeared on both Google and Amazon.
> 
> Customer: {customer_name}
> Issue: {main_complaint}
> Platforms: Google Reviews and {amazon / walmart / ebay}
> Product: {product_name}
> Your business type: {brand_owner / retailer / marketplace_seller}
> Cross-platform strategy: {same_response / customized_per_platform}
> Resolution offered: {refund / replacement / store_credit}
> 
> Write a 110 to 160 word response that works on both platforms, addresses the issue professionally, offers resolution, and includes contact information appropriate for both channels. Avoid platform-specific language that would seem odd on the other site.

**When to use it:** When angry customers post identical complaints across multiple review platforms to maximize damage to your reputation.

**Pro tip:** Respond on the platform where they seem most active first. Quick resolution there sometimes leads them to update or remove the duplicate review.
