## New Product Launches

> You are writing a Shopify product description for a just-launched item that needs to build excitement and trust.
> 
> Product: {product_name}
> Category: {product_category}
> Price point: {price_range: budget / mid-range / premium}
> Key benefit: {main_problem_it_solves}
> Unique features: {three_standout_features}
> Target customer: {specific_customer_type}
> Brand voice: {conversational / professional / luxury / playful}
> SEO keywords: {three_main_keywords}
> 
> Write a 280 to 320 word Shopify product description. Lead with the core benefit in the first sentence. Include all three features as separate short paragraphs. End with a confidence-building statement and soft urgency. Use the keywords naturally throughout. Format with bullet points for key specs.

**When to use it:** You've just added a new product to your catalog and need the description written before you announce it to your email list or social media.

**Pro tip:** Test two versions by changing the {brand_voice} variable. Conversational descriptions often convert 15-20% better than professional ones, even for B2B products.

## Competitive Differentiation

> You are writing a Shopify description for a product in a crowded market where differentiation is everything.
> 
> Product: {product_name}
> Main competitors: {two_main_competing_products}
> Your key differentiator: {what_makes_yours_different}
> Shared features: {what_competitors_also_have}
> Your unique features: {what_only_you_offer}
> Customer type: {who_switches_from_competitors}
> Proof point: {review_stat_or_test_result}
> Price positioning: {more_expensive / same_price / less_expensive}
> Brand promise: {what_you_guarantee_differently}
> 
> Write a 270 to 320 word differentiation-focused description. Don't mention competitors by name, but highlight what makes you different. Use "Unlike most..." or "While others..." phrasing. Include the proof point prominently. End with your unique brand promise.

**When to use it:** You're optimizing descriptions for products where customers are comparing multiple options and need a reason to choose yours.

**Pro tip:** Lead with your biggest differentiator in the product title itself, not just the description. If it's "30-day results guaranteed," make that part of the product name.

## SEO-Optimized Descriptions

> You are creating an SEO-focused Shopify product description that needs to rank for specific search terms.
> 
> Product: {product_name}
> Primary keyword: {main_keyword_phrase}
> Secondary keywords: {three_related_keyword_phrases}
> Search intent: {what_people_want_when_searching}
> Competitor ranking content: {what_currently_ranks_well}
> Product benefits: {key_customer_benefits}
> Technical specs: {important_specifications}
> Local relevance: {geographic_relevance_if_any}
> Brand name: {your_brand_name}
> Category: {product_category_for_schema}
> 
> Write a 350 to 400 word SEO-optimized description. Use the primary keyword in the first sentence. Include secondary keywords naturally throughout. Answer the search intent clearly. Add structured information for rich snippets. Keep it readable for humans first, search engines second. Include keyword variations in headers or bullet points.

**When to use it:** You've done keyword research and need descriptions that help your products show up in Google search results for your target terms.

**Pro tip:** Write the description for humans first, then go back and naturally weave in keywords. Forced keyword stuffing hurts both SEO and conversions in 2026.
