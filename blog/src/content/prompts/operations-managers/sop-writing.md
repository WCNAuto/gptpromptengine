## Equipment and Safety Procedures

> You are writing a safety SOP for equipment operation in a manufacturing environment.
> 
> Equipment: {equipment_name}
> Location: {facility_or_department}
> Primary hazards: {top_three_safety_risks}
> PPE required: {personal_protective_equipment_list}
> Authorized users: {who_can_operate}
> Emergency contact: {name_and_extension}
> Last incident date: {date_or_none}
> Compliance standard: {osha_iso_or_internal}
> 
> Write a 400 to 500 word equipment safety SOP. Start with a purpose statement and scope. Include pre-operation safety checks, step-by-step operating procedures with safety callouts, shutdown procedures, and emergency response steps. Use numbered lists for procedures and bullet points for safety warnings. End with training requirements and sign-off documentation.

**When to use it:** When safety incidents spike, new equipment arrives, or auditors are coming next week and you need documentation fast.

**Pro tip:** Add specific lockout/tagout steps even if they seem obvious. Auditors flag generic safety language but approve detailed procedures.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I customize these ChatGPT prompts for my specific industry requirements?
Replace the curly brace variables with your actual operational details, then add industry-specific compliance requirements or technical specifications in the constraints section. The prompts work across manufacturing, logistics, healthcare, and service operations with variable customization.

### What's the difference between using ChatGPT versus Claude for SOP generation?
ChatGPT handles structured procedures and technical documentation better, while Claude excels at conversational tone and complex reasoning. For operations SOPs, ChatGPT's formatting consistency and technical accuracy typically produce more usable first drafts.

### How do I ensure the AI-generated SOPs meet regulatory compliance requirements?
Add your specific regulatory standards (OSHA, ISO, FDA, etc.) as variables in the prompts, then have subject matter experts review AI outputs for technical accuracy and compliance gaps. Use the AI for structure and speed, but always validate against actual regulations.
