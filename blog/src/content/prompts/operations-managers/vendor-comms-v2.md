## Contract and Agreement Communications

> You are an Operations manager writing a contract amendment request to a key vendor.
> 
> Vendor: {vendor_company_name}
> Contact: {primary_contact_name}
> Original contract value: {contract_value}
> Amendment request: {specific_change_needed}
> Business justification: {reason_for_change}
> Timeline urgency: {urgent / standard / flexible}
> Relationship history: {strong / neutral / strained}
> Legal review status: {completed / pending / not_required}
> 
> Write a 250 to 350 word professional email requesting the contract amendment. Open with the specific change needed and contract reference. Include clear business justification in the middle section. Close with proposed next steps and timeline. Use collaborative language that preserves the vendor relationship while being direct about requirements.

**When to use it:** When scope changes, budget adjustments, or timeline shifts require formal contract modifications mid-project.

**Pro tip:** Always reference the original contract section number in your opening line—it signals you've done your homework and speeds vendor response time.

> You are an Operations professional responding to a vendor's contract renewal proposal that exceeds budget.
> 
> Vendor: {vendor_name}
> Current contract value: {current_annual_spend}
> Proposed increase: {percentage_or_dollar_increase}
> Budget constraint: {available_budget_amount}
> Service satisfaction level: {high / adequate / concerning}
> Alternative vendor options: {researched / limited / none}
> Renewal deadline: {specific_date}
> Negotiation history: {flexible / rigid / unknown}
> 
> Write a 300 to 400 word counter-proposal email. Start by acknowledging their proposal and expressing continued interest in partnership. Present budget constraints with specific numbers in the middle section. Close with a counter-offer that splits the difference or proposes modified service levels. Maintain professional tone while being firm on budget realities.

**When to use it:** When annual renewals come in over budget but you need to maintain the vendor relationship.

**Pro tip:** Include a specific counter-offer percentage in your initial response—vague "work with us" language prolongs negotiations unnecessarily.

## Performance Management Communications

> You are an Operations professional conducting a quarterly vendor performance review discussion.
> 
> Vendor: {company_name}
> Review period: {quarter_and_year}
> Overall performance rating: {exceeds / meets / below_expectations}
> Key metrics summary: {3_to_4_performance_indicators}
> Standout achievements: {specific_wins_or_improvements}
> Areas needing improvement: {2_to_3_specific_issues}
> Cost performance: {under / on / over_budget}
> Next quarter priorities: {upcoming_focus_areas}
> 
> Write a 350 to 450 word performance review summary email. Open with appreciation for partnership and overall assessment. Present metrics and achievements in structured format in the middle section. Address improvement areas constructively with specific examples. Close with forward-looking priorities and next review scheduling. Use balanced tone that maintains vendor motivation while addressing issues directly.

**When to use it:** During scheduled quarterly business reviews when you need to document performance and set expectations for the coming period.

**Pro tip:** Send performance data 48 hours before the review meeting—vendors come prepared with explanations and improvement plans instead of getting defensive.

> 
> Vendor: {company_name}
> Their stated reasons: {vendor_provided_excuses_or_explanations}
> Actual performance impact: {measurable_business_consequences}
> Pattern duration: {how_long_issues_have_persisted}

> Documentation level: {first_discussion / ongoing_issue / final_warning}

## Issue Resolution and Escalation

> You are an Operations manager escalating a vendor issue to senior leadership.
> 
> Vendor: {company_name}
> Issue summary: {critical_problem_in_one_sentence}
> Business impact: {operational_or_financial_consequences}
> Resolution attempts: {what_you_have_tried_already}
> Vendor response: {their_current_position_or_proposal}
> Timeline pressure: {deadline_or_urgency_factors}
> Escalation recipient: {senior_leader_name_and_title}
> Recommended action: {what_you_need_leadership_to_do}
> 
> Write a 300 to 350 word escalation email to senior leadership. Open with concise issue summary and immediate business impact. Document resolution attempts and vendor responses in the middle section. Close with specific recommendation for leadership action and timeline urgency. Use facts-focused tone that enables quick decision-making without emotional language.

**When to use it:** When vendor issues exceed your authority level or require senior leadership intervention for resolution.

**Pro tip:** Lead with dollar impact or customer consequence in your subject line—executives prioritize emails with clear business stakes over generic "vendor issue" subjects.
