## Performance Issues and Escalation

> You are a procurement operations manager addressing a vendor performance issue.
> 
> Vendor: {vendor_company_name}
> Contact: {vendor_contact_name}
> Issue: {specific_performance_problem}
> Impact: {business_impact_description}
> Previous discussions: {yes_no_when}
> Desired outcome: {specific_resolution_needed}
> Timeline: {deadline_for_resolution}
> Tone: {firm_but_professional / direct / collaborative}
> 
> Write a 250-300 word email that opens with the specific issue, quantifies the business impact, references any previous discussions, states exactly what you need resolved, and provides a clear timeline. Use a tone that maintains the relationship while making the urgency clear.

**When to use it:** When a vendor has missed SLAs, delivered poor quality, or caused operational disruption and you need immediate corrective action.

**Pro tip:** Always include specific metrics or examples in your {business_impact_description} variable - "3-day delivery delay affecting 40 customer orders" beats "causing delays."

> 
> Vendor: {vendor_company_name}
> Senior contact: {escalation_contact_name_title}
> Issue duration: {how_long_ongoing}
> Failed resolution attempts: {previous_attempts_summary}
> Current impact: {operational_business_impact}
> Contract terms: {relevant_sla_or_terms}
> Your company: {your_company_name}
> Your role: {your_title}
> Next steps needed: {specific_actions_required}
> 
> Write a 300-350 word escalation email that positions this as a partnership concern, not an attack. Reference the relationship value, summarize failed attempts to resolve at lower levels, state current impact clearly, and propose specific next steps with ownership.

**When to use it:** When operational-level discussions haven't resolved critical vendor issues and you need senior stakeholder intervention.

**Pro tip:** Lead with relationship value in escalation emails - mentioning contract size or partnership length early softens the escalation tone.
