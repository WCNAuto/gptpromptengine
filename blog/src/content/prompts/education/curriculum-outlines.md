## Unit Planning and Sequencing

> You are a curriculum designer creating a comprehensive unit outline for approval by department heads.
> 
> Subject: {subject_area}
> Grade level: {grade_level}
> Unit title: {unit_name}
> Duration: {number_of_weeks} weeks
> Key standards: {three_to_four_standards}
> Available resources: {textbook_materials_technology}
> Student demographics: {class_description}
> Assessment requirements: {required_assessments}
> 
> Write a 500-600 word unit outline including: learning objectives tied to standards, week-by-week progression of topics, three formative assessment checkpoints, one summative assessment, differentiation strategies for different learners, and required materials list. Use clear headings and bullet points for easy administrator review.

**When to use it:** When you're submitting unit plans for administrative approval and need comprehensive documentation.

**Pro tip:** Include specific page numbers or resource links in your materials list—administrators often check whether resources are actually available.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I modify these prompts for different grade levels without losing effectiveness?
Change the complexity of vocabulary and concepts rather than the structure. Elementary prompts need concrete examples and shorter time frames, while high school versions can include abstract thinking and longer-term projects. Keep the same variables but adjust expectations for student independence and prior knowledge.

### What's the fastest way to turn AI-generated curriculum outlines into working lesson plans?
Use the outline as your skeleton, then add specific activities, materials lists, and timing for each component. The AI gives you the scope and sequence—you add the practical classroom management details like transitions, grouping strategies, and backup activities for different pacing needs.

### How can I ensure AI-generated curriculum actually aligns with my state standards?
Always input your specific state standard codes and language into the prompts. After generating content, cross-reference each objective with your standards documents. AI is good at structure and organization but you need to verify the actual alignment matches your accountability requirements.
