## Weekly Status Updates

> You are writing a weekly project status report for stakeholders.
> 
> Project: {project_name}
> Project Manager: {your_name}
> Week ending: {date}
> Overall status: {green / yellow / red}
> Key accomplishments this week:
> {three_to_four_bullet_points}
> Challenges or blockers:
> {one_to_three_issues}
> Next week priorities:
> {two_to_four_priorities}
> Budget status: {on_track / over_budget / under_budget}
> Timeline status: {ahead / on_time / behind}
> Stakeholder attention needed: {yes_describe / no}
> 
> Write a 250-350 word professional weekly status report. Start with a clear status summary. Use bullet points for accomplishments and next steps. End with specific action items if stakeholder input is needed.

**When to use it:** Every Friday afternoon when you need to send weekly updates to your steering committee or sponsor.

**Pro tip:** If your status is yellow or red, lead with what you're doing to fix it, not just the problem itself.

> You are writing a project status update for a project that's behind schedule.
> 
> Project: {project_name}
> Current delay: {number_of_weeks_behind}
> Root cause of delay: {primary_reason}
> Impact on deliverables: {which_milestones_affected}
> Recovery plan: {your_mitigation_strategy}
> Additional resources needed: {people / budget / time / none}
> Revised completion date: {new_target_date}
> Risk to other projects: {yes_describe / no}
> Stakeholder: {name_and_role}
> 
> Write a 200-300 word status update that acknowledges the delay, explains the cause without making excuses, and presents a clear recovery plan. Use a problem-solution structure. Be direct but professional.

**When to use it:** When you need to report project delays to senior stakeholders who expect solutions, not excuses.

**Pro tip:** Always include what you've already done to address the delay, not just what you plan to do.

## Milestone Reporting

> You are writing a milestone report for a project that missed its target date.
> 
> Milestone: {milestone_name}
> Original due date: {planned_date}
> Actual completion: {actual_date_or_still_pending}
> Delay duration: {days_or_weeks_late}
> Reason for delay: {root_cause}
> Impact on project timeline: {downstream_effects}
> Impact on other milestones: {which_dates_shift}
> Recovery actions taken: {what_you_did}
> Lessons for future milestones: {process_changes}
> 
> Write a 250-350 word milestone delay report. Be factual about what happened without defensive language. Focus on impact assessment and corrective actions. Use a lessons-learned tone that shows accountability.

**When to use it:** When a critical milestone slips and you need to explain the situation to project sponsors.

**Pro tip:** Include one specific process change you're implementing to prevent similar delays on remaining milestones.

> Delivery date: {actual_date}
> Planned vs actual: {how_much_early}
> Success metrics: {quality_measures_exceeded}
> Stakeholder feedback: {client_or_user_response}
> Team performance highlights: {what_went_exceptionally_well}
> Cost performance: {budget_impact}
> Unexpected benefits: {bonus_outcomes}
> Application to future milestones: {what_to_replicate}
> 
> Write a 200-300 word success milestone report that celebrates achievement while capturing lessons learned. Balance recognition with practical insights for future phases. Use specific metrics and stakeholder quotes where possible.

**When to use it:** When your team delivers exceptional results and you want to document what worked for future projects.

**Pro tip:** Ask stakeholders for specific quotes about the delivery - their words carry more weight than your summary.

**Pro tip:** Give stakeholders a specific deadline for approval and explain exactly what happens to the timeline if they need longer.

## Stakeholder Communications

> You are writing a project status report for executives who need high-level information.
> 
> Project: {project_name}
> Strategic objective: {business_goal_this_supports}
> Current status: {green / yellow / red}
> Completion percentage: {percent_done}
> Key milestone this month: {major_deliverable}
> Business impact to date: {measurable_benefits_delivered}
> Risk to timeline: {none / minor / significant}
> Resource escalation needed: {yes_specify / no}
> Executive decision needed: {yes_what_decision / no}
> Bottom line: {one_sentence_summary}
> 
> Write a 150-200 word executive status report. Lead with business impact and strategic alignment. Use executive language focused on outcomes, not activities. End with clear ask if you need executive action.

**When to use it:** When reporting to C-level executives who need strategic context, not operational detail.

**Pro tip:** Never send executives a problem without proposing at least two solution options for them to choose from.
